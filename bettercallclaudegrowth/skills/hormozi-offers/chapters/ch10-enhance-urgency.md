# Chapter 10: Enhancing the Offer: Urgency

## Core Idea
Urgency limits TIME (a deadline), making prospects act NOW instead of later — it is the time-based counterpart to Scarcity (which limits supply). Deadlines drive decisions, so attach a real cut-off to your Grand Slam Offer and pair it with Scarcity for maximum pull.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Four Types of Urgency**: four ethical ways to limit *when* people can buy.
  - When to use: any offer that needs more people to take action now; combine one or more.
  - How (the four):
    1. **Rolling Cohorts (Cohort-Based Rolling Urgency)** — start clients on a regular cadence (e.g., a group that kicks off every Monday). "Sign up today and you're in the next group; otherwise you wait." The less frequent the kickoff, the stronger the pull. Bonus: choreographed onboarding that matters at scale.
    2. **Rolling Seasonal Urgency** — re-wrap the *same* core promotion under a new seasonal name with a real start/finish date (New Year Promo → Valentine's Promo → Spring Special). Display the live dates everywhere; spin up a fresh campaign/landing page in ~5 minutes when one ends.
    3. **Pricing or Bonus-Based Urgency (Promotional / Pricing Deadlines)** — make the *promotion, discount, or bonuses* the thing they could miss, not the service itself. Lets year-round sellers use urgency honestly ("not sure how long we'll run this; we change them every ~4 weeks").
    4. **Exploding Opportunity** — the *opportunity itself* decays with time (arbitrage, early-platform advantage, exploding job offers). Every second of delay = disproportionate gains lost. Emphasize the decay.

## Key Concepts
- **Urgency**: limiting *time* via a deadline so action happens now; "Scarcity is a function of quantity. Urgency is a function of time."
- **Rolling Cohorts**: starting new clients in groups on a fixed cadence, creating a natural "next start date" deadline.
- **Rolling Seasonal Urgency**: recurring promotional windows with seasonal names giving each a genuine start and finish.
- **Pricing/Bonus Deadline**: urgency built from a changing price, discount, or swappable bonus (free install, free onboarding, extra workshop) rather than the offer itself.
- **Exploding Opportunity**: an arbitrage-style chance whose value erodes over time, forcing fast decisions.
- **Deadlines drive decisions**: the operating principle — a real cut-off is what converts intent into action.
- **Pipeline cleaning**: announcing a real price increase ("price is going up, get in now") to convert fence-sitters and pull cash forward.

## Mental Models
- **Think of Urgency as the time-twin of Scarcity** — Scarcity caps *how many*, Urgency caps *when*. Run them together for compounding effect; this chapter separates them only to teach.
- **Use the "last-4-hours" truth to kill your fear of deadlines** — in a week-long campaign, 50–60% of sales land in the final ~3% of the time. People who "miss out" on a deadline were never going to buy anyway, so a real deadline makes you *more* money, not less (same logic as Guarantees).
- **Use a new "wrapper" when the core offer is unchanged** — re-naming the same promotion by season manufactures a legitimate differentiator with novelty plus a built-in expiry; the #1 strategy for local businesses, who must vary marketing more often than national advertisers.
- **Make deadlines real, always** — fake countdowns destroy credibility and make you look like "every other wannabe marketer."

## Anti-patterns
- **Phony or fake deadlines/countdowns**: erodes trust and brands you as a wannabe; the timer must be genuine.
- **Fearing lost sales from turning business away**: unfounded — you gain from the many who act and lose only people who'd never buy anyway.
- **Lying about the service deadline**: a roofer can't honestly refuse a customer who buys after the date; create urgency around the *promotion/price/bonus*, not the service, to keep integrity.
- **Raising prices silently**: always announce increases — it signals strength and pulls forward cash from fence-sitters.

## Key Takeaways
1. Attach a real deadline to your offer; pick at least one of the four urgency types and stack with Scarcity.
2. Default to Rolling Cohorts — they create urgency *and* a repeatable, scalable onboarding cadence.
3. For year-round / honest-deadline businesses, attach urgency to the promotion, discount, or bonus, not the core service.
4. Seasonal re-wrappers are the highest-ROI urgency lever for local businesses — minutes of editing, conversions through the roof.
5. Trust the last-hours data: deadlines net more revenue, so stop fearing the "lost" sales.
6. Spot Exploding Opportunities (arbitrage, early-mover, exploding offers) and emphasize their time-decay to force fast decisions.
7. Keep every deadline real to protect credibility.
