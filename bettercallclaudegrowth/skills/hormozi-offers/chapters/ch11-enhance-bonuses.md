# Chapter 11: Enhancing the Offer: Bonuses

## Core Idea
A single offer is worth less than the same offer broken into its component parts and presented as a stack of named **Bonuses** — because each bonus widens the **Price-to-Value Discrepancy** by adding value instead of cutting price. Never discount the core offer; pile on bonuses until the stack alone dwarfs the price and "snaps the rubber band" holding the wallet in the prospect's pocket.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Bonuses (the value-stacking enhancer)**: Take the deliverables you assembled in **Trim & Stack** and re-present them one at a time as discrete, named bonuses rather than one lumped offer. The infomercial logic: establish the core price, then "but wait, there's more" until passing feels stupid.
  - When to use: After the core offer is built, at the point of presentation/close — when you want to raise perceived value without touching price.
  - How: Anchor the stated price to the core offer, then layer increasingly valuable bonuses so the discrepancy grows wider and wider.

- **The Bonus Bullets (how to present each bonus)**: A fixed checklist for presenting every bonus so it lands:
  1. Always offer them (use the bulleted bundle from the Trim & Stack section).
  2. Give it a special name with a **benefit in the title**.
  3. Tell them: (a) how it relates to their issue, (b) what it is, (c) how you discovered it / what it took to create, (d) how it specifically improves their life — faster, easier, less effort/sacrifice (**Value Equation**).
  4. Provide proof it's valuable (stat, past client, or personal experience).
  5. Paint a vivid mental image of life already using it and enjoying the benefit.
  6. Always ascribe a price tag and justify it (**price-anchor** each bonus).
  7. Prefer tools & checklists over additional trainings (lower effort/time = higher value per the Value Equation).
  8. Each bonus should crush a specific obstacle/concern in the prospect's mind (prove their limiting belief wrong).
  9. Or solve the next problem they'll logically hit — before they even encounter it.
  10. Total bonus value should eclipse the core offer's value (and subconsciously implies the core must be even more valuable).
  11. Add Scarcity and Urgency to the bonuses themselves to put the technique "on steroids."

- **Advanced Bonuses — Other People's Products & Services**: Get non-competing businesses to give you their products/services as bonuses in exchange for free exposure to your high-quality customers.
  - When to use: When you want to inflate offer value at near-zero cost and build referral/affiliate revenue.
  - How: Solicit free sessions/discounts (e.g., chiropractor adjustments, gym membership, pharmacy discounts) → stack them so their value alone exceeds your price → then go "Jedi": negotiate a group discount for clients PLUS an affiliate commission paid to you per referral, turning each bonus into an indirect revenue stream.

## Key Concepts

- **Price-to-Value Discrepancy**: The gap between what they pay and what they perceive they get; bonuses widen it by raising value, never by cutting price.
- **Price Anchoring**: Stating the core offer price first, then expanding value around it so each bonus feels like found money.
- **Product Anchoring**: Lesser-known cousin of price anchoring — positioning bonuses so the core product seems even more valuable by comparison.
- **Wow Factor**: The test for what becomes a bonus vs. core — pull out the most distinct, standalone, short-but-high-value nuggets (checklists, infographics) so they don't get lost in the bundle.
- **Reciprocity**: People struggle to reject a gift; adding bonus after bonus to accommodate makes them feel almost obligated to buy.
- **Bonus Vault**: A back-pocket library of recorded workshops, webinars, events, interviews, checklists, scripts, and templates — created once, given infinitely — to sprinkle into offers to close deals.
- **True-to-price bonuses**: Partner bonuses with real, defensible market value (e.g., "$100 chiropractor adjustment") that justify your entire price in savings alone.

## Mental Models

- **Think of bonuses as a rubber band**: Each added bonus stretches the value-vs-price tension wider until it snaps and the prospect buys. The core offer is the anchor point; bonuses do the stretching.
- **Use "add a bonus," not "drop the price," every time you're closing**: Discounting teaches customers your prices are negotiable (weakness); adding value to close keeps you in a position of strength and goodwill.
- **Think tools/checklists/templates over more training**: Anything that cost you time or money once but can be handed out endlessly with low recipient effort is the perfect bonus — the Value Equation rewards low time and effort.
- **Treat each bonus as an obstacle-killer**: Map a bonus to each "why I can't/won't succeed" belief, so the stack systematically dismantles every objection before it's spoken.

## Anti-patterns

- **Discounting the core offer to close**: Erodes perceived value and trains customers to negotiate; add bonuses instead.
- **Listing everything as one undifferentiated offer**: Value that isn't enumerated is unknown — un-stacked deliverables get no credit for their worth.
- **Giving bonuses without a name, reason-why, proof, or price tag**: An unnamed, unquantified bonus reads as filler, not value.
- **Burying the Wow Factor item inside the core bundle**: Distinct high-value nuggets lose their punch when mixed in; isolate and highlight them.
- **Revealing bonuses before the ask (in 1-on-1 selling)**: In a 1-on-1, ask for the sale first; reveal bonuses after a yes as a "wow," or one-at-a-time to overcome a specific objection if they say no.

## Reference Tables

**Bonuses with Scarcity vs. Urgency (subtle but real difference)**

| Type | Mechanism | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Scarcity (not time-bound) | Bonuses available only through this program, nowhere else | "Only people who sign up get Bonus #1, 2, 3 — never sold or available anywhere else" / "3 tickets left to my $5,000 event — buy and claim one" |
| Urgency (time-bound) | Buy TODAY or lose the bonus | "Buy today and I'll add the $1,000 XYZ bonus free — to reward action-takers" |

**Partner-Bonus Economics (the $400 pain-clinic example)**

| Mechanism | Effect on a $400 offer |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Stacked partner bonuses (adjustments, massages, training, discounts) | Bonus value alone exceeds the $400 price |
| Affiliate commissions per referral (chiro $100, orthotics $100, pharmacy $100, etc.) | Up to ~$350 extra per customer — pure profit |
| Group discount + commission to yourself (the Prestige Labs model) | Client saves 30%, you/athlete earn 40% of net sales — win-win-win |

## Key Takeaways
1. Break the offer into parts and present each as a named bonus — the stacked version always out-sells the lumped one.
2. When closing, never cut price; add bonuses. It raises value AND keeps you in a position of strength.
3. Run every bonus through the Bonus Bullets: name it with a benefit, give a reason-why, prove it, price-anchor it, and tie it to an obstacle or the prospect's next problem.
4. Make total bonus value eclipse the core offer — it both widens the Price-to-Value Discrepancy and implies the core must be even more valuable.
5. Favor tools, checklists, swipe files, scripts, and templates (and recorded events) — high perceived value, near-zero marginal cost, low recipient effort.
6. Solicit non-competing partner businesses for bonus products/services in exchange for exposure; then negotiate group discounts and affiliate commissions so bonuses become revenue streams.
7. Stack Scarcity and Urgency onto the bonuses themselves to amplify the effect.
