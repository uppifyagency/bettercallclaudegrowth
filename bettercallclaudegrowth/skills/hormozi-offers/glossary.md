# Glossary — $100M Offers (Alex Hormozi)

**Anti-Guarantee** — A guarantee where you explicitly state "all sales are final," backed by a compelling "reason why" (e.g., the product is too exposing/copyable once seen); used for consumables or proprietary content (Ch 12).

**Bonuses** — Component parts of your offer broken out and stacked as named add-ons to widen the price-to-value discrepancy; always add bonuses rather than discounting the core offer (Ch 11).

**Brick Exercise** — A 120-second divergent-thinking drill: list as many uses for a brick as possible to train your brain to find many solutions to one problem (Ch 6).

**Commodity** — A product available from many places, prone to price-driven (not value-driven) purchases; "if all products are equal, the cheapest wins" (Ch 2).

**Conditional Guarantee** — A guarantee with terms and conditions, ideally "better than money back," often tied to key actions the client must take to succeed (Ch 12).

**Container Word** — The MAGIC component denoting the offer is a bundled system (e.g., Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Accelerator) (Ch 13).

**Convergent Thinking** — Problem solving with many known variables converging on a single right answer (think math); how school teaches us (Ch 6).

**Create Flow, Monetize Flow, Then Add Friction** — Mantra: generate demand first, get yeses, and only then optimize/reduce what you offer (Ch 8).

**Divergent Thinking** — Generating many possible solutions to one problem under dynamic conditions; how life pays you and how you build a Grand Slam Offer (Ch 6).

**Dream Outcome** — Top value-driver (goal: increase); the gap between the prospect's current reality and their envisioned feelings/experience; sell "the vacation, not the plane flight" (Ch 5, Ch 7).

**Effort & Sacrifice** — Bottom value-driver (goal: decrease); the tangible and intangible "other costs" a client incurs along the way; why done-for-you costs more than DIY (Ch 5).

**Exploding Opportunity** — Urgency type: an arbitrage/time-decaying opportunity where waiting costs the prospect disproportionate gains (Ch 10).

**Grand Slam Offer** — An incomparable offer combining attractive promotion, unmatchable value proposition, premium price, and unbeatable guarantee (plus a money model), letting you sell in a "category of one" (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Grow or Die** — Core tenet that every person/company is either growing or dying; "maintenance is a myth" (9% market growth = baseline) (Ch 2).

**Gross Profit** — Revenue minus the direct cost of servicing an additional customer (Ch 2).

**Honest Scarcity** — The most ethical scarcity: truthfully advertising how close you are to your self-defined capacity (e.g., "81% full") (Ch 9).

**Hormozi Law** — "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make." / "The longer the runway, the bigger the plane that can take off." (Ch 11).

**Implied Guarantee** — Any performance-based structure (revshare, profit-share, triggers, ratchets) where "if I don't perform, I don't get paid" (Ch 12).

**Lifetime Value (LTV / LTGP)** — Gross profit accrued over the entire lifetime of a customer (gross profit × number of purchases) (Ch 2).

**Logical vs Psychological Solutions** — Logical fixes have usually already been tried; the remaining gains come from psychological solutions (e.g., a dotted train-arrival map instead of faster trains) (Ch 5).

**MAGIC Formula** — Naming formula: Make a Magnetic "Reason Why," Announce your Avatar, Give them a Goal, Indicate a time Interval, Complete with a Container word; use 3–5 components (Ch 13).

**Market (Health, Wealth, Relationships)** — The three eternal markets; pick a growing sub-niche within one (Ch 3).

**Money Model** — The payment terms within a Grand Slam Offer that let you get paid to acquire customers, removing the cash constraint on growth (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Never Be a Commodity** — The principle of differentiating so you sell on value, escaping the price-driven "race to the bottom" (Ch 2).

**Niche Slap** — Coined term/warning against newer entrepreneurs hopping niche to niche instead of committing once they pick (Ch 3).

**Normal Market** — A market growing at the marketplace rate with unmet needs in health, wealth, or relationships; sufficient for success (avoid only "bad"/shrinking markets) (Ch 3).

**Offer** — The goods/services you provide, how you accept payment, and the terms; the lifeblood that initiates the value exchange (Ch 1).

**Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — Top value-driver (goal: increase); the prospect's belief that the offer will actually work for them ("people pay for certainty") (Ch 5).

**Perception Is Reality** — Value is only created when the prospect *perceives* the increased likelihood, decreased time delay, and decreased effort (Ch 5).

**Price-to-Value Discrepancy** — The gap between what a prospect pays (price) and what they get (value); buyers buy to "get a deal," so widen this gap by adding value, not cutting price (Ch 4, Ch 11).

**Riches Are in the Niches** — Principle that niching down lets you charge up to 100x more for essentially the same product (Ch 3).

**Sales to Fulfillment Continuum** — The tradeoff between ease of sales and ease of fulfillment; the goal is the sweet spot that sells well and is easy to deliver (Ch 8).

**Scarcity** — Limiting *quantity* (supply of slots, bonuses, or "never again") to trigger fear of missing out and raise prices/demand (Ch 9).

**Solutions Delivery Vehicles ("The How")** — Step 4 of offer creation: brainstorming every possible way to deliver each solution, varied by attention level (1-on-1 / small group / one-to-many) and effort level (DIY / DWY / DFY) (Ch 8).

**Starving Crowd** — The most important success lever: an overwhelming market demand; ranks above offer strength and persuasion skills (Market > Offer > Persuasion) (Ch 3).

**Stacking Guarantees** — Combining multiple guarantees (e.g., an unconditional 30-day plus a conditional 90-day) to further reverse risk (Ch 12).

**Status** — The deeper driver beneath most dream outcomes; frame benefits in terms of perceived standing relative to others (Ch 5).

**Three Levers on Success** — Order of importance: Starving Crowd (market) > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills (Ch 3).

**Time Delay** — Bottom value-driver (goal: decrease); time between purchase and receiving the promised benefit; create fast early "emotional wins" (Ch 5).

**Trim & Stack** — Step 5 of offer creation: trim delivery vehicles to keep only high-value/low-cost (and high-value/high-cost) items, then stack the survivors into one high-value deliverable (Ch 8).

**Unconditional Guarantee** — The strongest guarantee; a no-questions-asked / satisfaction refund "trial where they pay first"; best for lower-ticket offers (Ch 12).

**Urgency** — Limiting *time* (deadlines) to lower the prospect's action threshold; methods include Rolling Cohorts, Rolling Seasonal, Pricing/Bonus-based, and Exploding Opportunity (Ch 10).

**Value Equation** — The master formula: (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice); drive the top up and the bottom toward zero for near-infinite value (Ch 5).

**Virtuous Cycle of Price** — Raising prices increases client emotional investment, perceived value, results, client quality, and your margin (and vice versa when you cut prices) (Ch 4).

**Wantrepreneur** — A person full of ideas and unrealized potential who hasn't yet taken the entrepreneurial leap (Ch 1).

**"Wrapper" / Offer Fatigue** — Renaming and re-creative-ing the same core offer (not changing the offer itself) to refresh response and get leads forever; change creative → copy → headline → duration → enhancer last (Ch 13).
