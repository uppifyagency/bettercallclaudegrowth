# Patterns — $100M Offers (Alex Hormozi)

Repeatable techniques and step-by-step playbooks for building a Grand Slam Offer.

## Pick a Starving Crowd (Choosing a Market)
**When to use**: Before building any offer — market choice outranks offer and persuasion (Starving Crowd > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills). You channel demand, never create it.
**How**: Evaluate any candidate market against 4 indicators:
1. **Massive Pain** — they desperately need (not just want) the solution; "the pain is the pitch."
2. **Purchasing Power** — they can afford the price you need to charge.
3. **Easy to Target** — reachable via lists, groups, associations, channels.
4. **Growing** — a tailwind, not a shrinking/dying market.
Anchor in the 3 durable markets that always exist (always tremendous pain): **Health, Wealth, Relationships**. Find a growing sub-niche inside one with buying power and easy targeting.
**Trade-offs / Notes**: A "normal" market (growing at market rate) is fine; only a "bad"/shrinking market kills everything. Commit to the niche ("don't make me niche slap you") — riches are in niches; you can charge up to 100x more for the same product niched down (e.g., $19 generic course → $1,997 for shift-nurses).

## Tune the Value Equation
**When to use**: To quantify and raise the perceived value of any offer so you can charge premium prices.
**How**: Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Perceived Time Delay × Perceived Effort & Sacrifice).
1. **Dream Outcome (increase)** — depict their desired status/feeling; frame benefits as status gained in others' eyes.
2. **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (increase)** — proof, track record, guarantees, social proof ("people pay for certainty").
3. **Perceived Time Delay (decrease)** — shorten time-to-result; engineer fast early emotional wins ("fast beats free").
4. **Perceived Effort & Sacrifice (decrease)** — make it seamless, done-for-you, immediate.
**Trade-offs / Notes**: Perception is reality (London dotted map > faster trains). Focus on the bottom (time + effort) — top-side claims are easy/lazy and less unique. Get the bottom toward zero = near-infinite value. Use psychological over logical solutions.

## Virtuous Cycle of Price (Raise Prices)
**When to use**: Whenever tempted to compete on price; ~99% of businesses need to raise prices, not lower them.
**How**:
1. Never set price by copying the market (those competitors are broke); there's no benefit to being second-cheapest, only to being most expensive.
2. Raise price only AFTER sufficiently increasing value so it's still "money at a discount" (e.g., $100k value for $10k).
3. Charge enough that it "stings" — those who pay most pay most attention, get invested, and get better results.
4. Reinvest the multiplied margin into experience, talent, and scale.
**Trade-offs / Notes**: Raising price raises emotional investment, perceived value, results, client quality, and margin (the wine-tasting effect — higher price literally raises perceived value). Requires conviction earned through delivery; product must deliver.

## Grand Slam Offer Part I — Dream Outcome → Problems → Solutions
**When to use**: First half of building the core offer (use divergent thinking — many answers; do the Brick Exercise to warm up).
**How**:
1. **Identify the Dream Outcome** — sell the vacation, not the plane flight (e.g., "Lose 20 lbs in 6 weeks": big outcome + short time delay).
2. **List every Problem** — write each thing the customer must do (before, during, after), in sequence, in insane detail; for each, list the friction across all 4 value drivers (Dream Outcome / Likelihood / Effort & Sacrifice / Time). Expect dozens (16 core × sub-problems → 32–64).
3. **Reframe into Solutions** — turn each problem into solution language: add "How to" + reverse the problem (e.g., "buying healthy food is expensive" → "How to buy healthy food for less than your current grocery bill"). Then name each solution.
**Trade-offs / Notes**: You will solve EVERY problem — a single unsolved "one thing" loses sales (the eating-out guide story). The more problems, the more value you can create.

## Grand Slam Offer Part II — Delivery Vehicles, Sales-to-Fulfillment, Trim & Stack
**When to use**: Second half — turn the solutions list into a profitable, scalable, irresistible deliverable.
**How**:
- **Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum**: find the sweet spot where it's both easy to sell and easy to fulfill. Mantra: "Create flow. Monetize flow. Then add friction." Over-deliver wildly at first, then optimize with the cash.
- **Step 4 — Brainstorm Delivery Vehicles ("The How")**: list every conceivable way to deliver each solution. Use the **Product Delivery Cheat Codes**: (1) level of personal attention (1-on-1 / small group / one-to-many); (2) level of effort expected (DIY / DWY / DFY); (3) live medium (in-person, phone, email, text, Zoom, chat); (4) recording format (audio/video/written); (5) response speed/hours; (6) the 10x-to-1/10th test.
- **Step 5a — Trim**: list cost-to-you of each. Remove high-cost/low-value first, then low-cost/low-value. Keep only (1) low-cost/high-value and (2) high-cost/high-value. Favor high-value "one-to-many" assets (big cost-value gap; like software).
- **Step 5b — Stack**: bundle remaining items into one high-value deliverable; present as Problem → Solution Wording → Sexier Bundle Name, with the actual delivery vehicles underneath; ascribe a $ value to each (total value should dwarf the price, e.g., $4,351 value for $599).
**Trade-offs / Notes**: The bundle must (1) solve all perceived problems, (2) give you conviction it's one-of-a-kind, (3) be impossible to compare to competitors. Create assets once; they generate margin for years.

## Scarcity (Decrease Supply)
**When to use**: To raise prices and indirectly increase demand via exclusivity. "When demand increases, cut supply." Keep supply under generated demand.
**How** — pick the type that fits your model:
- **Physical products**: limited releases (flavors/colors/quantity) — always sell out, and announce the sellout (social proof). ~Once a month.
- **Services** (3 caps): (1) **Total Business Cap** — only X clients total, waitlist, periodically open 10–20%; (2) **Growth-Rate Cap** — only X new clients per week; (3) **Cohort Cap** — only X per class/cohort on a set cadence.
- **Honest Scarcity** — just announce real capacity ("81% to capacity this week").
**Trade-offs / Notes**: Three forms: limited seats/slots, limited bonuses, never-available-again. Always keep fewer spots than you can sell so you sell out fast. Extreme scarcity: tiny-cap, very high-priced 1-on-1 access. Optional "once you're out you can never come back."

## The 4 Types of Urgency (Limit Time, Not Quantity)
**When to use**: To lower the action threshold and drive faster decisions ("Deadlines drive decisions"). Often paired with scarcity.
**How**:
1. **Cohort-Based Rolling Urgency** — "Sign up today to join Monday's group, else wait for the next kickoff."
2. **Rolling Seasonal Urgency** — same core promo re-wrapped by season with a real countdown/end date (New Year, Valentines, etc.). #1 strategy for local businesses.
3. **Pricing or Bonus-Based Urgency** — the discount/bonus (not the service) expires; lets year-round sellers create FOMO honestly. Use price changes to clean the pipeline.
4. **Exploding Opportunity** — the external arbitrage/opportunity itself decays with time (crypto, new ad platform, exploding job offer).
**Trade-offs / Notes**: Fear of losing sales by turning business away is unfounded — 50–60% of campaign sales come in the last hours; deadline-skippers usually weren't going to buy. Countdowns must be REAL or you lose credibility.

## Bonuses Playbook (Stack Value, Never Discount)
**When to use**: To widen the price-to-value discrepancy by adding value instead of cutting price. A single offer is worth less than the same offer broken into stacked, named bonuses.
**How** — bonus bullets:
1. Always offer them. 2. Give each a benefit-driven name. 3. Tell them: how it relates to their problem, what it is, how you discovered/created it, how it improves their life (faster/easier/less effort). 4. Provide proof. 5. Paint a vivid post-use mental image. 6. Always ascribe a price tag and justify it. 7. Tools/checklists > extra trainings (lower effort = higher value). 8. Each bonus crushes a specific objection. 9. Solve their next problem before they hit it. 10. Total bonus value should eclipse the core offer. 11. Add scarcity/urgency to bonuses themselves.
- **Selling 1-on-1**: ask for the sale FIRST; reveal bonuses after the yes (wow effect). If no, add a bonus matching their objection, then ask again ("fair enough?").
- **Advanced**: source bonuses as Other People's Products/Services (free to you, exposure for them); negotiate group discounts + a referral commission so bonuses become profit streams.
**Trade-offs / Notes**: Never discount the core offer (it teaches negotiability and signals weakness). Information products, tickets, and recorded workshops are ideal high-perceived-value, low-cost bonuses — keep a "vault."

## The 4 Types of Guarantees (Reverse Risk)
**When to use**: Risk is the #1 objection; reversing it is the single biggest conversion lever (can 2–4x conversion). Spend as much time on the guarantee as on deliverables.
**How** — structure as a conditional: "If you don't get X result in Y time, we will Z." The four types:
1. **Unconditional** — pay-first trial / no-questions-asked refund (full, 50%, refund + ancillary costs, pay for competitor, money + $1,000). Strongest pull; best for lower-ticket B2C.
2. **Conditional** — terms attached, ideally "better than money back" and tied to the success-driving actions (outsized/double-triple refund, service guarantee = work free until result, modified service, credit-based, personal service, hotel+airfare, wage-payment, release of service, delayed second payment, first-outcome). Best for higher-ticket/B2B.
3. **Anti-Guarantee** — "all sales are final," with a compelling reason-why (damaging admission of exposure); for consumable or once-seen-can't-unsee products.
4. **Implied** — performance models: revshare, profit-share, ratchets, bonuses/triggers — "if I don't perform, I don't get paid." Best alignment; needs measurable outcomes + collection.
**Trade-offs / Notes**: Do the math — closing 130% as many with refunds doubling 5%→10% still nets 1.23x. You can STACK guarantees (e.g., unconditional 30-day + conditional triple-money-back 90-day). Name it creatively. Guarantees enhance — they can't fix a bad product/sales team. High-cost fulfillment → prefer conditional or anti-guarantee.

## The MAGIC Naming Formula
**When to use**: To name the offer (and sub-items/bonuses) so the right avatar self-selects; naming alone can 2–10x response. Re-name to refresh fatigued offers without changing the offer.
**How** — combine 3–5 of these 5 components (order flexible, shorter/punchier wins):
- **M — Make a Magnetic "Reason Why"** (Attention): Free, 88% off, Grand Opening, Anniversary, New Year, Spring.
- **A — Announce Your Avatar** (Discrimination): be specific/hyper-local — "Lakeway Moms," "Salon Owners," "Brick & Mortar Businesses."
- **G — Give Them a Goal** (Purpose): the dream outcome — Pain Free, Celebrity Smile, Double Your Profit, First Client.
- **I — Indicate a Time Interval** (Timeline): 4-Hour, 21-Day, 6-Week, 3-Month (avoid quantifiable-claim-plus-duration where platforms ban implied guarantees).
- **C — Complete With a Container Word** (Method): Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Incubator, Masterclass, Accelerator, Sprint, System, Reset.
Examples: "Free Six-Week Lean-By-Halloween Challenge"; "5 Clients in 5 Days Blueprint"; "Fill Your Gym in 30 Days (Free!)."
**Trade-offs / Notes**: Optionally rhyme or alliterate (don't force it). Test 2–3 names, keep the control, test new challengers. When offers fatigue, change in this order: (1) creative, (2) body copy, (3) headline/wrapper, (4) duration, (5) free/discount enhancer, (6) monetization structure — lower = more operational drag, change last.
