# $100M Offers — Cheatsheet

> Goal: make offers "so good people feel stupid saying no." Raise price only after value is raised.

## The Value Equation

```
        Dream Outcome  ×  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
        Time Delay  ×  Effort & Sacrifice
```

**Increase the top two, decrease the bottom two.** Drive the bottom toward zero → value approaches infinite. Perception is reality. Pros compete on the bottom (immediate, seamless, effortless).

| Driver | Goal | Buyer asks |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | ↑ | What will I get? |
| Perceived Likelihood | ↑ | Will it work for me? |
| Time Delay | ↓ | How long? |
| Effort & Sacrifice | ↓ | What's expected of me? |

## Market Selection

**Starving Crowd — 4 indicators:** (1) Pain (massive, urgent) · (2) Purchasing Power · (3) Easy to Target · (4) Growing.

**3 durable markets:** Health · Wealth · Relationships. Niche down within one.

**Priority:** `Starving Crowd (Market) > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills`. Never a *bad* (shrinking) market; *normal* is fine. Pick, then commit ("don't make me niche slap you").

## Pricing — Virtuous Cycle of Price

Charge a **premium** (heinous amounts). Higher price → bigger margin → more value delivered → better results → more allure. **Never discount** (race to commodity efficiency = slavery). Be so much higher buyers think "something different must be going on here."

## Build the Grand Slam Offer

| # | Step | Do |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Dream Outcome** | Identify the gap between their reality & dream |
| 2 | **Problems** | List every obstacle before/during/after (use 4 value drivers; aim for 16+) |
| 3 | **Solutions** | Reverse each problem into "how to ___"; name them |
| 4 | **Delivery Vehicles** | List every way to solve each (the "how"); favor high-value 1-to-many |
| 5 | **Trim & Stack** | Cut high-cost/low-value, then low-cost/low-value; stack the rest into one bundle |

## The 5 Enhancement Levers

**1. Scarcity (limit supply):** Limited Slots/Seats · Limited Bonuses · Limited Supply of physical goods. Sell out consistently (~once/month). Cohort Caps. Use honest scarcity only.

**2. Urgency (limit time) — 4 types:**
| Type | Mechanic |
|---|---|
| Cohort-Based Rolling | Starts on fixed cadence; "next cohort kicks off Monday" |
| Rolling Seasonal | Real seasonal/holiday deadlines that reset |
| Promotional / Pricing | Price or bonus deadline for buying now |
| Exploding Opportunity | The opportunity itself expires |

**3. Bonuses (stack value):** Each ascribed its own price; bonuses' total value should *eclipse* the core offer. Address a specific obstacle/objection. Tools & checklists beat extra trainings (lower effort/time). Add scarcity/urgency to bonuses. Use other people's products/services (free to you).

**4. Guarantees (reverse risk) — 4 types:**
| Type | What it is |
|---|---|
| Unconditional | "No questions asked" refund — pay first, try, refund anytime. Strongest, riskiest |
| Conditional | "If you do X & don't get Y, we Z" — terms tied to key actions; make it *better than money back* |
| Anti-Guarantee | "All sales are final" — needs a strong reason why; for exclusive/copyable goods |
| Implied | Performance-based: revshare, profit-share, ratchets, triggers — no result, no pay |
*Do the math (more sales usually offsets more refunds). Name it boldly. Can stack guarantees.*

**5. Naming — M-A-G-I-C** (use 3–5; any order; short & punchy):
| | Component | One-liner |
|---|---|---|
| **M** | Magnetic "Reason Why" | Why this offer / what's in it for me (Free, % off, Grand Opening) |
| **A** | Announce Avatar | Call out who it's for; hyper-local beats city |
| **G** | Give a Goal | Their dream outcome, specific & tangible |
| **I** | Indicate Interval | Time to result (4-Hour, 21-Day, 6-Week) |
| **C** | Container word | Bundle/system name (Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Accelerator) |
*Rhyme or alliterate (nice-to-have). Change wrapper to refresh — never the core offer. Fatigue order: creative → copy → headline → duration → enhancer → money model.*
