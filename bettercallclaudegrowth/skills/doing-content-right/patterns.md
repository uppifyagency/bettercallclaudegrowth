# Patterns & Techniques — Doing Content Right

Concrete, repeatable plays from the book. Each: **When** to reach for it, **How** to run it.

## Positioning & Niche (Ch 1)

## Saturation Reframe
**When**: judging whether a space is "too crowded."
**How**: measure your *likelihood to out-innovate*, not the headcount of competitors. 1M travel bloggers is fine if none serve the audience the way you can.

## "What for who" One-liner
**When**: defining or sharpening a publication.
**How**: write one sentence = biggest pain you solve + your differentiator vs. the closest competitor + the core audience. If you can't, you're not ready to publish.

## "Most ___ blog I know" Test
**When**: finding your edge.
**How**: make readers fill in a single adjective about you. If no word surfaces, there's nothing to spark word-of-mouth — sharpen until one does.

## Start Specific, Then Expand
**When**: scoping your audience.
**How**: begin narrow (easier to target and rank), broaden later. Shrinking an audience is far harder than growing one.

## Audience Cross-check
**When**: validating a niche before committing.
**How**: confirm demand via search volume + existing newsletters/blogs/communities + whether the market is growing.

## Building the Home (Ch 2)

## Define Non-negotiables First
**When**: at launch or before a pivot.
**How**: list everything you want, resolve conflicts, circle the few non-negotiables, set guardrails around them.

## Choose a Good-enough Stack
**When**: starting out (don't over-engineer).
**How**: cheap domain (Namecheap/Porkbun), Ghost or WordPress.org, any ESP that fits budget. Spend days, not weeks — "a name may make you but won't break you."

## Build a Writing Habit
**When**: starting out.
**How**: schedule reps, write what you know, and keep every published piece above your threshold of excellence.

## Distribution (Ch 3)

## Explore and Exploit
**When**: deciding where to invest.
**How**: run 1–3 channels in 2–4 week cohorts, cut non-fits, double down on winners, and tier channels 0–3 by payoff.

## Do Things That Don't Scale
**When**: under ~100 fans.
**How**: reach out 1:1, reply to every comment, leverage weak ties. Manual now buys compounding later.

## Diversify to Become Antifragile
**When**: one channel dominates your traffic.
**How**: balance across channels that score differently on CODES — you own nothing but your newsletter.

## Match Copy to Platform Vibe
**When**: posting on viral/community channels.
**How**: matter-of-fact titles on HN; post full value (not just a link) with a soft CTA on Reddit; never beg for upvotes.

## Optimize Subscribe Modals
**When**: converting readers to subscribers.
**How**: value-aligned copy + social proof + calm objections + remove distractions; mix event-triggered, fixed, and landing-page captures.

## Referral + Giveaway Loops
**When**: accelerating newsletter growth.
**How**: make sharing one-click with tiered rewards; gate giveaway entries behind referrals to keep quality high.

## SEO (Ch 4)

## Validate Intent Before Keyword Selection
**When**: before choosing any keyword.
**How**: Google it, inspect the top 3 results' type. Long-form only ranks for *informational* intent.

## Target Long-tail Over High-volume
**When**: starting or on a low-DA domain.
**How**: aim ~200–5,000 searches/month and pick keywords you can realistically rank #1 for (0 × 1M = 0).

## Build 30 Links to Cross DA ~10
**When**: the domain is "fresh."
**How**: own linkable assets, do interviews/HARO, build a free tool, claim unlinked mentions — ~30 links unlocks Google's retest ("Second Chances").

## On-page Checklist
**When**: a good keyword is chosen.
**How**: primary keyword in title/meta/slug/body, secondary keywords sprinkled, title ≤60 chars, meta ≤160, length matched to the problem. Then Search Console → Request Indexing; set canonical.

## Target Associated Problems
**When**: no one searches for your product or name.
**How**: rank for the adjacent problems your audience already searches (Transferwise ranking on routing-number pages).

## Re-optimize Old Content
**When**: periodically.
**How**: bucket articles informational vs. inspirational, re-target keywords on the informational ones, prioritize by volume, refresh for "freshness."

## Monetization (Ch 5)

## SWEAT Assessment
**When**: choosing/sequencing revenue methods.
**How**: score each on Scalability, Work, Audience, Timeline before committing.

## Trust-to-method Mapping
**When**: picking a lead monetization method.
**How**: monetize the perception you already hold — opinion→affiliates, demonstrated ability→course, entertainment→sponsored copy.

## Newsletter Sponsorship Pricing
**When**: setting ad rates.
**How**: price = a metric you control (clicks/opens) × a multiplier set by value delivered; calibrate by reaching out to 5–10 sponsors for real data.

## Promote Page
**When**: fielding inbound sponsor demand.
**How**: build a Stripe-Checkout media-kit page with direct pay links to capture passive demand.

## Pre-sell to De-risk Products
**When**: unsure people will buy.
**How**: pre-sell on Gumroad/PayPal before building; let real money validate.

## Let the Market Decide Pricing
**When**: pricing digital products.
**How**: start low, raise on a schedule until conversion dips, then hold.

## Mindset (Ch 6)

## Default to Action / See-200-feet-ahead
**When**: overwhelmed by a big project, or facing a reversible (Type 2) decision.
**How**: don't over-plan; take the next concrete step (send 5 emails), commit to at least one step per day, let momentum compound.

## Request Filter ("How to Say No")
**When**: growth brings more requests than you can serve.
**How**: keep responding but say no; screen by asking for questions up front or charging via Patreon/booking page — charge *before* you feel ready.
