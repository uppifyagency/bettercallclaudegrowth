# Cheatsheet — Doing Content Right

One-page quick reference. The whole book is a pipeline:
**Personal Monopoly → Build the Home → Distribute → SEO → Monetize → (avoid the mistakes).**

## The 5-Stage Content Engine

| Stage | Question it answers | Headline framework |
|-------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 1. Personal Monopoly | What can *only I* do? | Idea Ikigai, Diffusion of Innovation |
| 2. Build Your Home | Where do I publish? | The Unwritten Contract, blog **+** newsletter |
| 3. Distribution | How do people find it? | Brick by Brick (6 channels), Explore & Exploit |
| 4. SEO | How does Google find it? | CRU, intent before keywords |
| 5. Monetization | How does it pay? | SWEAT, trust-to-method mapping |

## Niche Validation (Ch 1)
A space is worth entering if you can answer YES to **either**:
- **The new** — a relevant problem nobody serves yet, OR
- **The old, 1% better** — an existing problem you can solve measurably better.
> Saturation = your odds of out-innovating, *not* the number of competitors.

## CODES — score every distribution channel (Ch 3)
**C**ost · **O**wnership · **D**ependability · **E**ffort · **S**calability
> You own nothing but your newsletter — diversify to stay antifragile.

## The 6 Channel Types — "Brick by Brick" (Ch 3)
Bedrock · Viral · Social · Syndicate · Targeted · Paid
- **Bedrock** (SEO, newsletter, Quora) = depend on these.
- **Viral** (HN, Reddit) = post on, never depend on.

## SEO Decision Order (Ch 4)
1. **Intent** — Google the term, read top 3 results' type (long-form ranks only for *informational*).
2. **Volume** — target long-tail ~200–5,000/mo you can actually rank #1 for.
3. **Competitiveness** — check keyword difficulty (rivals' DA + backlinks) in Ahrefs.
4. Write to **CRU** (Credible, Relevant, Usable) → on-page checklist → Request Indexing.
> Fresh domain? Build ~30 links to cross **DA 10** and unlock Google's "Second Chances" retest.

## Monetization — pick with SWEAT (Ch 5)
Score each method on **S**calability · **W**ork · **A**udience · **T**imeline.

| Method | Best when |
|--------|-----------|
| Affiliates | You're trusted for opinions/recommendations |
| Newsletter sponsorships | Engaged list; price = metric × multiplier |
| Digital products | You can package a Personal Monopoly; pre-sell first |
| Services/consulting | Early, low audience, high-touch, non-scalable |
| Ads | Large traffic, low effort, low RPM |
> Pricing: **let the market decide** — start low, raise until conversion dips. Price on *perceived value*, not effort.

## Painkiller Test (Ch 2 & 5)
Paid content must be an **aspirin** (relieves a felt pain people will price) — not a **vitamin** (nice-to-have). If it's "more of the same," don't gate it.

## The 3 Biggest Mistakes (Ch 6)
1. **"If you write it, they will come"** — distribution is not optional.
2. **Getting too micro** — don't optimize pixels before you have readers.
3. **Not defaulting to action** — for reversible (Type 2) decisions, ship.

## Decision rule that runs through the book
**Type 1 (irreversible)** → plan carefully. **Type 2 (reversible)** → act fast, look ~200 feet ahead, one step a day.
