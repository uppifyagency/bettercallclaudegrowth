# Glossary — Doing Content Right

Key terms from Steph Smith's *Doing Content Right*, alphabetical. `(Ch N)` marks where it's covered.

- **100 True Fans** — the small core audience worth obsessing over; reach and serve them before chasing scale (Ch 1, Ch 6)
- **Aspirin not a Vitamin / Painkiller vs. Vitamin** — content people *need* (relieves a felt pain, has pricing power) vs. content that's merely nice-to-have (Ch 2, Ch 5)
- **Associated Problems** — when no one searches for your product/name, rank for the adjacent problems your audience already searches for (Ch 4)
- **Backlink / Referring Domain** — an inbound link that acts as a micro-trust signal; unidirectional (Ch 4)
- **Bedrock content / channels** — durable, evergreen, compounding foundations (SEO, your newsletter, Quora) that keep delivering without a publishing treadmill (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 4)
- **Best of Both Worlds** — keep a free list for growth and gate select content for revenue; expect 3–10% free→paid conversion (Ch 2)
- **Brick by Brick** — the six channel types you stack: Bedrock, Viral, Social, Syndicate, Targeted, Paid (Ch 3)
- **Build in the Open** — share goals and struggles publicly so others help you ("pushing your car along the road") (Ch 3)
- **Canonical tag** — HTML signal marking the original of syndicated/duplicate content so it isn't outranked (Ch 4)
- **Catchability** — how well traffic from other channels converts into newsletter subscribers (the net, not just the fish) (Ch 3)
- **Channel risk** — whether you can profitably *reach* the people who care (vs. Market risk) (Ch 1)
- **CODES** — lens for evaluating a distribution channel: Cost, Ownership, Dependability, Effort, Scalability (Ch 3)
- **The Contract / Non-negotiables** — the implicit promises to your audience; most at risk when you start monetizing (Ch 2, Ch 5)
- **CPC / CPM / CPO / CPS** — sponsorship pricing metrics ordered from closest to the advertiser's goal (clicks/opens/orders) to furthest (sends) (Ch 5)
- **Create Value > Capture Value** — give openly and build trust first; extract revenue later (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 5)
- **CRU** — content must be Credible, Relevant, and Usable to rank and retain readers (Ch 4)
- **Default to Action** — for reversible decisions, act now and look only a few steps ahead (Ch 6)
- **Diffusion of Innovation curve** — model of how ideas spread (innovators → laggards); you can start a *new* curve in a niche even if blogging overall is mature (Ch 1)
- **Distribution vector** — a scalable sharing mechanism baked into the content itself (Ch 1)
- **Diversification** — a foundation of many small income streams, tested until one hits (Ch 5)
- **Domain Authority (DA/DR)** — logarithmic score of a site's relative strength from its backlink profile (Ch 4)
- **Explore and Exploit** — test 1–3 channels in short cohorts, cut what doesn't fit, double down on winners; tier them 0–3 (Ch 3)
- **Follow vs. Nofollow** — follow links pass authority ("juice"); nofollow tells Google to disregard the vote (Ch 4)
- **Good Problems to Have** — challenges that only appear *because* your impact is growing: Losing Your Time, Edge, Why (Ch 6)
- **Hollow metrics** — surface numbers (raw followers/list size) that look impressive but signal little (Ch 3)
- **Idea Ikigai** — the intersection of what's interesting, doesn't yet exist, and where you have unique exposure (Ch 1)
- **Infinite leverage** — the written word reaches globally at near-zero marginal cost — but 0 × any audience is still 0 (Ch 1)
- **Informational vs. Inspirational (Viral)** — informational content wins search; inspirational/viral content wins social shares (Ch 3, Ch 4)
- **Keyword difficulty** — how hard a SERP is to rank on, driven by competitors' DA and backlinks (Ch 4)
- **K-factor (Viral Coefficient)** — how many new readers each reader brings; > 1 means organic growth (Ch 1)
- **Link juice** — authority passed through a follow link (Ch 4)
- **Long-tail keyword** — a lower-volume, specific query that's far easier to rank #1 for (Ch 4)
- **Loss leader** — an asset sold at a loss to acquire a customer you recoup later (Ch 3, Ch 5)
- **Market risk** — whether people actually care about what you make (vs. Channel risk) (Ch 1)
- **Niche** — *targeted*, not small; your defensible corner of the internet (Ch 1)
- **Owned vs. Rented land** — your newsletter/site is owned; social followings are rented and can vanish (Ch 2, Ch 3)
- **Perceived value** — psychological, monetary, functional, and social value — set price by this, not by your effort (Ch 5)
- **Personal Monopoly** — a skill, experience, or understanding only you (or very few) can deliver; your strongest moat (Ch 1)
- **The Power Law** — online attention is non-linear; a tiny fraction of content captures most of the reward (Ch 1)
- **Products with Duality** — bundling formats (e.g. ebook + live sessions) to raise the price ceiling (Ch 5)
- **Promote page** — a modern media kit with direct Stripe pay links that captures inbound sponsor demand (Ch 5)
- **Publication** — operating *both* a blog (discoverable) and a newsletter (owned) as one system (Ch 1, Ch 2)
- **Search intent** — the bond between a query and the content that satisfies it; the non-negotiable precursor to keyword choice (Ch 4)
- **Search volume** — monthly searches for a keyword, reframed as "volume of value" (Ch 4)
- **Second Chances** — once a fresh domain crosses ~DA 10 (≈30 links), Google retests and re-ranks it (Ch 4)
- **SERP** — search engine results page; its shape reveals the dominant intent (Ch 4)
- **SMART goals** — Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-bound — best set as *inputs* you control (Ch 3)
- **SWEAT** — score a monetization method on Scalability, Work, Audience, Timeline before committing (Ch 5)
- **Table stakes** — being "better" is the price of entry to be seen, not a differentiator (Ch 1)
- **Things that don't scale** — manual, 1:1 tactics to win your first ~100 fans (Ch 3)
- **Threshold of excellence** — a quality bar below which you publish nothing (Ch 2)
- **Trust-to-monetization mapping** — monetize through the perception you've earned (opinion→affiliates, ability→course, entertainment→ads) (Ch 5)
- **Type 1 vs. Type 2 decisions** — Type 1 = irreversible (plan hard); Type 2 = reversible (act fast) (Ch 2, Ch 6)
- **The Unwritten Contract** — define your non-negotiables up front; breaking them costs trust (Ch 2)
- **Weak ties** — casual acquaintances who surface novel opportunities more often than close friends (Ch 3)
- **"What for who" one-liner** — one sentence: biggest pain solved + your differentiator + core audience (Ch 1)
