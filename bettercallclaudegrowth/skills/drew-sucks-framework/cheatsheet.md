# Cheatsheet — The SUCKS Framework (Kieran Drew)

## The Pre-Publish Run-Through
Whip out something you've written and ask:
- [ ] **S** — Can I be more **specific**?
- [ ] **U** — Is it **unique** (personal perspective) and **useful**? Is it **undeniable**?
- [ ] **C** — Is it **clear**, **curious**, and **conversational**?
- [ ] **K** — Is it **kept simple**?
- [ ] **S** — Is it well **structured**?

> For anything longer than an email, aim to tick every box. Even a few tweaks make all the difference. This is the 80:20 of copywriting — the rest is reps.

## SUCKS Decoded
| Letter | Question | Core moves |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **S** Specific | Is it specific? | Rule of One · Quantify (non-round numbers) |
| **U** | Unique / Useful / Undeniable? | How-To→How-I · Unique Mechanisms · Proof Stack |
| **C** | Clear / Curious / Conversational? | "What do I mean?" edit · 4 curiosity triggers · write to your Gran |
| **K** Kept simple | Is it kept simple? | Small words · 1/3rd Rule · Hemingway grade 4 |
| **S** Structured | Is it structured? | Bullets/white space/headings · slippery slope · vary sentence length |

## The Persuasion Proof Stack (weak → strong)
1. Authority by Association (studies, stats, endorsements)
2. Your Results (show, don't tell)
3. Customer Results (testimonials, case studies) ← strongest

## The 4 Curiosity Triggers
1. What they want · 2. What they didn't know · 3. Open loops · 4. Stories

## The 3 Editing Tips
1. Hemingway app — never above **grade 4**
2. Change screen, change state (laptop→phone, morning→post-gym)
3. **1/3rd Rule** — delete a third before publishing

## Quick Rules
- Don't edit and write at the same time — wait **24 hours**.
- Small word > big word. One word > two words.
- "Would I say this in person?" If not, rewrite.
- Read it **out loud** before publishing — we hear what we read.
- The magic happens at the end — structure to get people there.

## Author's North Star
Persuasion at scale = business leverage. "An empty calendar is the true definition of success."
