# Chapter 3: U — Be Useful and Unique

## Core Idea
"Your bank account is directly related to how much pain you can solve." Make your writing genuinely useful — and make it unique, because if someone else could have written it, they already have.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Useful + make-sure-they-know-it**: solve a real problem AND signal the payoff up front.
  - When to use: every piece — lead with the value, don't bury it.
  - How: contrast "Here's a story about my life" (useless/generic) with "This lesson will destroy any doubts about your self-worth" (useful/specific payoff).
- **How To's → How I's**: write from personal perspective so every piece helps the reader know you a little better.
  - When to use: turning generic advice into relatable, fan-building content.
  - How: (1) Turn "How To" into "How I"; (2) start every piece with a tidbit of your story. You're not the hero — the reader is — but drop "daily breadcrumbs about who you are" so they can relate.
- **Unique Mechanisms**: build named frameworks — "take something old and create something new." The SUCKS framework itself is an example.
  - When to use: when you want to be un-copyable and memorable.
  - How: package an existing idea into your own branded system. "People are always looking for new solutions to old problems."

## Key Concepts
- **Unique mechanism**: a branded framework that makes your idea ownable (copywriting term).
- **Breadcrumbs**: small personal details dropped consistently that build fandom.
- **The Useful/Unique matrix**: Useless↔Useful × Generic↔Unique → aim for Useful + Unique ("Gold"); avoid Useless+Generic ("Trash").

## Mental Models
- Think "**desire is suffering, so the internet is a cesspit of pain**" — your job is to solve it.
- If it's copyable, it's already been written — **uniqueness is survival**.

## Anti-patterns
- **Diary-entry writing**: making yourself the hero instead of the reader.
- **Generic, copyable content**: indistinguishable from everyone else → ignored.

## Key Takeaways
1. Solve real pain and make the usefulness obvious.
2. Convert How-To's into How-I's; open with a slice of your story.
3. Build unique mechanisms (named frameworks) to become un-copyable.
