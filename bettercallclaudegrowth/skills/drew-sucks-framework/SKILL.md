## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The SUCKS Framework (the master checklist)
A pre-publish audit. Draft freely, then run any piece through six questions:

- **S — Specific?** Vagueness makes readers uncertain, and uncertain people don't act.
- **U — Unique, Useful, Undeniable?** Solve real pain, be un-copyable, and prove it.
- **C — Clear, Curious, Conversational?** Easy to understand, impossible to stop reading, sounds human.
- **K — Kept simple?** With endless noise, the simplest writer wins.
- **S — Structured?** "You gotta look good to get read."

> It's the **80:20 of copywriting** — "the rest is just reps." The name is sticky on purpose: "the idea was to suck people in." For anything longer than an email, aim to tick every box.

### S — Specificity (Ch 2)
- **Rule of One**: per piece, pick **One True Fan, One problem, One solution, One story**. "Write to everyone = write to no one."
- **Quantify**: specific, non-round numbers create authority and belief. "I help people lose weight" → "56 clients, avg 21lbs, under 4 months."

### U — Useful + Unique (Ch 3)
- Solve real pain AND signal the payoff. Reader is the hero, not you.
- **How To's → How I's**: write from personal perspective; drop daily breadcrumbs so readers relate.
- **Unique Mechanisms**: build named frameworks (like SUCKS) — "take something old, create something new."

### U — Undeniable (Ch 4)
- The real marketing question isn't "What's in it for me?" but **"Why should I believe you?"**
- **Persuasion Proof Stack** (weak→strong): Authority by Association → Your Results → **Customer Results** (strongest). Build a habit to save feedback, testimonials, case studies.

### C — Clear (Ch 5)
- "Your job is not to say smart things. It's to learn smart things and make them easy to understand." Cleverness serves your ego; clarity serves the reader.
- Edit ≠ write — wait **24 hours**. Then line by line: "What do I mean?" → say that instead.

### C — Curious (Ch 6)
- **4 Triggers**: (1) what they want, (2) what they didn't know, (3) open loops, (4) stories.
- Keep a **Surprise Document**. Niche down. "The best storyteller wins."

### C — Conversational (Ch 7)
- Write to **one familiar person** (best friend / Gran). Pre-publish: "Would I say this in person?" — if not, rewrite until it sounds like you.

### K — Kept Simple (Ch 8)
- Small word > big word; one word > two. Simple ≠ shallow (see Feynman, Paul Graham).
- **3 Editing Tips**: (1) Hemingway, never above grade 4; (2) change screen/state between writing & editing; (3) **1/3rd Rule** — delete a third before publishing.

### S — Structured (Ch 9)
- Format for the **skim**: bullets, white space, short paragraphs, headings → a **slippery slope** to the end (where the buying happens).
- **Vary sentence length**; read aloud (or record yourself). "We hear what we read."
