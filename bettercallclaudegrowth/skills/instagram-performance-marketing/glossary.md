# Glossary — Instagram Performance Marketing

**10k-Impression Kill Rule** — If a campaign still underperforms after roughly 10,000 impressions, turn it off because it is unlikely to improve. (Ch 10)
**50 Conversions / 7 Days** — The threshold the algorithm must reach (at least 50 conversion events within seven days) to exit the learning phase. (Ch 10)
**A/B testing** — An experimental method that pits two or more versions of an ad, page, or campaign against each other to see which performs better. (Ch 15)
**Above the fold** — The screen region visible without scrolling, where the headline and CTA should be placed. (Ch 8)
**Accelerated Ad Spend** — Ramping up budget on a campaign that has cleared the early-success CPA bar to push it through learning faster. (Ch 10)
**Actionable metrics** — Goal-aligned metrics such as CTR, conversion rate, cost per conversion, and ROI used to evaluate a test. (Ch 15)
**Ad fatigue** — Decline in ad performance when an audience sees the same creative too long, requiring a creative refresh. (Ch 7, Ch 13)
**Ad relevance / message match** — Keeping ad copy consistent with the landing page or content shown after the click. (Ch 12)
**Advantage Campaign Budget** — Meta's option to spread one budget across multiple ad sets at once, left off because parallel ad sets compete for the same audience and raise CPM. (Ch 11)
**Audience Manager** — Meta's tool where you upload a customer list (whole or segmented) to create custom audiences and lookalikes. (Ch 14)
**Audience Network** — Meta's off-platform placement network, deselected because it lowers conversions. (Ch 3, Ch 11)
**Audience size** — The Lookalike/expansion breadth expressed as a percentage of the seed, trading similarity (small %) for reach (large %). (Ch 5, Ch 14)
**Audience+ (with Audience Suggestions)** — Meta's audience-expansion tool used with the optional suggestions box to add interests, job titles, and demographic targeting beyond your base audience. (Ch 11)
**Automated Outreach** — Using technology to deliver personalized messages at scale so every lead receives timely, relevant communication without manual effort. (Ch 9)
**Awareness / Reach objectives** — Objectives focused on maximum impressions regardless of likelihood to act, carrying the lowest CPM. (Ch 4)
**Behavioral Trigger** — An automation rule fired by a specific lead action (visit, open, download) that launches a personalized follow-up. (Ch 9)
**Benefits over features** — Framing copy around how the offer improves the reader's life rather than what the product technically is. (Ch 12)
**Bounce rate** — The share of paid visitors who leave immediately, the primary symptom of a misaligned or unoptimized page. (Ch 8)
**Campaign Objective** — The goal set for a Meta campaign that controls which users Meta delivers ads to based on their past ad-engagement behavior, driving CPM, CPA, and CTR. (Ch 3, Ch 4)
**Cart Abandoner** — A user who added items and reached checkout but left without buying, the highest-intent retargeting segment. (Ch 13)
**Chatbot** — An AI-powered agent that engages leads in real-time conversations, answers FAQs, and qualifies leads 24/7. (Ch 9)
**Conversion Location** — Where a user lands after clicking the ad (usually the website); its options change by objective and it strongly affects CPM. (Ch 11, Ch 17)
**Conversion Objective** — The campaign goal selected in Meta that dictates which account types are targeted and has the largest single impact on performance. (Ch 2)
**Conversion Rate** — The percentage of clicks that resulted in a desired action such as a sale or lead. (Ch 16)
**Conversions objective** — A Meta objective that drives specific actions on your site or app such as purchases, sign-ups, or downloads, with the highest typical CPM. (Ch 4)
**Converted Audience (1,000 events)** — The terminal milestone where a campaign reaches its lowest achievable CPA after 1,000 conversion events. (Ch 17)
**Cost Per Click (CPC)** — How much you pay for each click on your ad; also a bidding option. (Ch 16)
**Cost per website registration** — The amount paid to acquire one sign-up; in the case study it moved from $94 to $37 to $0.74. (Ch 1)
**CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)** — The cost to acquire one converting customer, the core outcome metric these chapters aim to minimize. (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 5, Ch 17)
**CPM (Cost per 1,000 Impressions)** — The auction cost to reach 1,000 people and the main driver of CPA; pushed up by objective/conversion location and down by geo and lookalike expansion. (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 4, Ch 6, Ch 11, Ch 14, Ch 16, Ch 17)
**CTA-objective alignment** — Matching the call to action to the campaign goal (purchase CTA for sales, sign-up CTA for leads). (Ch 12)
**CTR (Click-Through Rate)** — The percentage of people who clicked your ad after seeing it (Meta average ~0.90%, up to ~9.8% with AI UGC-style video). (Ch 3, Ch 16, Ch 17)
**Custom Audience** — An audience built from your own uploaded data or behavior/engagement signals, used as a targeting source or Lookalike seed. (Ch 3, Ch 5, Ch 13, Ch 17)
**Custom Location Groups** — User-built sets of countries or cities matched to specific market research. (Ch 6)
**Customer Journey Automation** — Mapping and automating the touchpoints a lead encounters through the funnel to deliver tailored, behavior-based experiences. (Ch 9)
**Customer Lifetime Value** — The total value a nurtured lead delivers over time, increased by fostering loyalty and repeat business. (Ch 9)
**Defining Early Campaign Success** — The rule to accelerate spend only on a campaign whose CPA is within 50%–60% of your target acquisition cost. (Ch 10)
**Delivery by past engagement** — Facebook profiles users by what they historically do after seeing ads and shows them objective-matched ads (link-clickers get Traffic ads, form-fillers get Lead ads, purchasers get Sales ads). (Ch 4)
**Dynamic Ads** — Ads that automatically show users the exact products or personalized content they previously interacted with. (Ch 7, Ch 13, Ch 16)
**Dynamic Creative** — Meta feature that auto-recombines creative elements, turned off because it adds variability and can hurt performance. (Ch 11, Ch 12)
**Dynamic adjustment** — Meta's capability to reallocate more impressions to a better-performing variation while a test is still running. (Ch 15)
**Dynamic text insertion** — Auto-personalizing headline or copy with the user's name or location. (Ch 7)
**Emerging markets** — Low-competition, fast-growing, high-engagement regions (e.g. India, Indonesia, Nigeria) with CPMs a fraction of US/EU rates. (Ch 6)
**Emotional hook (Love Potion)** — A copy element designed to trigger one specific emotion (curiosity, FOMO, excitement, relief) because buying decisions are emotional first. (Ch 12)
**Engagement automations** — Automated follow-up flows triggered the instant a lead engages an ad or fills a lead form, pushing them toward a conversion goal. (Ch 3)
**Engagement rate after sign-up** — A post-conversion quality signal; the winning campaign beat all other channels on engagement after registration. (Ch 1)
**Engagement-based lookalike audience** — A lookalike built from ad or video engagement to expand reach and lower CPM. (Ch 7)
**Frequency Cap** — A Meta setting limiting how often a person sees a retargeting ad; suggested 3–5 times per week. (Ch 13)
**Front-Loading Ad Spend** — Concentrating the majority of budget early (after initial low-cost conversions) to exit the learning phase quickly, treated as an investment. (Ch 2, Ch 10)
**Geo expansion** — Adding many converting countries to a campaign to dilute auction competition and lower CPM. (Ch 6)
**Google Search Audience Feed** — A third-party-purchased list of people who recently searched Google for chosen keywords, uploaded to Meta as a high-intent Custom Audience to seed lookalikes. (Ch 3, Ch 5, Ch 14, Ch 17)
**Gradient overlay technique** — A gradient placed over the bottom half of a video so static text on top stays readable. (Ch 7)
**Growth Hacking** — Low- or no-budget acquisition craft learned before having ad spend; the precursor to paid performance marketing. (Ch 1)
**High Intent Audience** — Consumers recently searching Google for your keywords, captured as a search audience and uploaded to Meta. (Ch 2)
**iFrame Issues** — Breakage of login or sign-up forms inside the Facebook/Instagram in-app browser that silently kills conversions. (Ch 2)
**Impressions** — The number of times your ad is shown to people (counts repeat views). (Ch 16)
**Interest layering** — Combining/stacking interests to reach a more targeted audience that converts to revenue at higher quality. (Ch 1)
**Interest targeting** — Selecting Meta audiences by their stated hobbies or interests using Meta's audience filters. (Ch 1, Ch 5)
**Landing page** — The destination page where the ad's promise is fulfilled or broken; the bridge between the ad and the desired customer action. (Ch 8)
**Lead Capture and Segmentation** — Collecting lead data from Meta ad interactions and grouping leads by demographics, interests, and behaviors as the foundation of journey automation. (Ch 9)
**Lead Scoring** — Assigning numeric value to leads based on engagement levels to prioritize high-potential prospects and allocate resources. (Ch 9)
**Learning Phase** — Meta's optimization period that needs roughly 50 conversion events before delivery stabilizes and CPM/CPA begin dropping. (Ch 2, Ch 10, Ch 11, Ch 17)
**Localization** — Adapting language, currency, and imagery to each market instead of reusing one creative everywhere. (Ch 6)
**Location-Based Targeting** — Meta option to target by intent of presence: people living in, recently in, or traveling in a location. (Ch 6)
**Lookalike Audience** — A Meta-generated audience of accounts that resemble a seed source across demographics, interests, and behavior, widening reach and lowering CPM. (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 5, Ch 12, Ch 13, Ch 14, Ch 16, Ch 17)
**Meta A/B testing** — Meta's data-driven, AI/ML-powered testing that evaluates many variables at once and dynamically shifts delivery toward the better performer in real time. (Ch 15)
**Meta Pixel** — A code snippet on your site that tracks visited pages and viewed products, powering Website Visitor retargeting audiences. (Ch 13)
**Mirroring** — A landing-page technique used to decrease bounce rate and increase page-goal conversions. (Ch 3)
**Multivariate testing** — Testing combinations of multiple variables simultaneously, contrasted with A/B testing which isolates a single element. (Ch 15)
**Original Audience** — The Ad Set option (vs Advantage+) that lets you manually pick interests and job titles rather than letting Meta auto-select. (Ch 17)
**Performance Goal (Maximize Number of Conversions)** — The default ad-set delivery objective kept selected so Meta optimizes toward completed conversion events. (Ch 11)
**Performance marketing** — Running paid ads where every dollar is justified by a measurable result (registration, subscriber, or sale) rather than impressions or brand vanity. (Ch 1)
**Personalized Content Delivery** — Using captured lead data to serve content matched to each segment's specific needs and interests. (Ch 9)
**Purchase intent** — A buying signal (like an active Google search for a buying keyword) that makes a person higher-value seed data than an interest match. (Ch 5)
**Radius targeting** — Drawing a circle (1 to 50 miles) around a point to reach only people who can physically show up. (Ch 6)
**Re-Engagement Audience by Video Watch Time** — A retargeting pool built from how long viewers watched a video ad, available only when running video creative. (Ch 2)
**Reach** — The number of unique individuals who have seen your ad. (Ch 16)
**Reader-as-hero narrative** — Body copy structured as a story where the reader overcomes their problem with the product as the enabling tool. (Ch 12)
**Recommended Daily Budget** — A suggested $500/day at launch to maximize impression velocity and surface early signs of traction. (Ch 10)
**Regional Targeting** — Meta's pre-grouped country bundles such as North America, European Union, and Emerging Markets. (Ch 6)
**Relevance score** — Meta's signal that rewards audience-relevant ads with lower CPMs. (Ch 6)
**Resonance** — Tuning creative to what makes the audience feel and click while feeding the algorithm engaging, high-quality content. (Ch 7)
**Retargeting** — Serving ads to people who already showed interest (visited, clicked, added to cart) but did not convert, to bring them back. (Ch 13)
**Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)** — Revenue generated per dollar of ad spend, without which you cannot sustainably keep running ads. (Ch 1, Ch 16)
**Revenue-mapped conversion** — A conversion that ties to actual revenue (a paid subscriber), as distinct from a cheap top-funnel registration that may never pay. (Ch 1)
**ROI** — Return on investment, calculated as campaign revenue divided by its cost; positive ROI means the campaign is profitable. (Ch 14)
**Sales / Catalog Sales objective** — Objectives that show ads to users most likely to purchase using past purchase history, with Catalog Sales promoting products from a catalog. (Ch 4)
**Sample size** — The audience volume a test needs; too small a sample produces skewed, unreliable data. (Ch 15)
**Seed Audience** — The source group of already-interested people (emails, phone numbers, or website visitors) Meta uses as the template to build a lookalike; its quality determines what the algorithm learns to optimize toward. (Ch 5, Ch 14)
**Special Ad Category** — Restricted ad verticals like housing or credit with limited targeting options requiring separate optimization handling. (Ch 11)
**Spotlight/magnifying-glass effect** — Meta ads concentrate attention on the page, so an unoptimized page has its flaws magnified rather than hidden. (Ch 8)
**Statistical significance** — The confidence threshold a result must meet before a winner is declared, preventing premature conclusions. (Ch 15)
**Sterile Audience** — A deliberately constrained audience that limits cross-ad-set competition so early test results are not skewed. (Ch 11)
**Stupid tax** — Money lost paying for lessons through trial and error while learning which ad settings and audiences work. (Ch 1)
**Symbiotic relationship** — The mutual amplification between ads and landing pages: the ad brings traffic and the page converts it, neither performing well alone. (Ch 8)
**Target CPA (Cost Per Action)** — A bidding option that optimizes ad delivery toward conversions. (Ch 16)
**Test duration** — The time a test runs to gather meaningful data, typically 7 to 14 days depending on audience size and ad frequency. (Ch 15)
**Thumb-friendly design** — Sizing CTA buttons and interactive elements large enough to be easily tapped with a thumb on mobile. (Ch 8)
**Trust signals** — Credibility elements such as testimonials, reviews, awards, and security badges that reduce visitor hesitation. (Ch 8)
**UGC video ads** — User-generated-content-style video creative (makeable with generative AI, e.g. Captions.ai) that looks like a real person talking about the product and can lift CTR substantially. (Ch 2, Ch 7)
**Unique Value Proposition (UVP)** — What clearly sets your product apart and makes it desirable, communicated in the title. (Ch 12)
**User value (performance marketing)** — Users more likely to perform your conversion objective are worth more, so the marketer pushes ads toward the highest-value, most-likely-to-act users. (Ch 4)
**Visual hierarchy** — Deliberate use of contrasting colors, fonts, and sizes to guide the visitor's attention through the page. (Ch 8)
**Watch-time retargeting** — Building retargeting audiences from people who watched a set percentage of a video (e.g. >25%) but did not convert. (Ch 7)
**2.5-second rule** — The window a headline has to grab a fast-scrolling user before they move on. (Ch 12)
