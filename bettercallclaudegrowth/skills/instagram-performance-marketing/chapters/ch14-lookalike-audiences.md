# Chapter 14: Look-a-Like Audiences

## Core Idea
Meta Lookalike Audiences take the demographics, interests, and behaviors of a "seed audience" (your existing/high-intent customers) and find strikingly similar strangers across Facebook and Instagram. Because they widen your targeting, they lower CPM and therefore your Cost per Acquisition while expanding reach to people likely to convert.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **5-Step Lookalike Creation Process**: The author's explicit workflow for building and running a lookalike audience.
  - When to use: Whenever you want to scale beyond interest targeting to a larger, conversion-likely audience at lower CPM.
  - How:
    1. **Define your seed audience** — a group that has already shown interest (email addresses, phone numbers, website visitors). Larger and more relevant = more accurate lookalike.
    2. **Choose your lookalike audience size** — bigger reaches more people but they resemble the seed less; pick a size matching your goal.
    3. **Create the lookalike audience** — Meta finds similar people; this can take up to 24 hours.
    4. **Use the lookalike audience** — apply it to ad targeting for retargeting, prospecting, or brand awareness.
    5. **Measure success** — track reach, impressions, clicks, and conversions.

- **Audience Size Tiers (% of seed)**: How "tight" vs "broad" the match is, framed as a percentage of the seed audience size.
  - When to use: To trade similarity for scale based on campaign goal.
  - How:
    - **Small (1–10%)**: target people very similar to the seed.
    - **Medium (11–50%)**: target people somewhat similar.
    - **Large (51–100%)**: target people broadly similar.

- **Four Success Metrics**: The author's evaluation set for lookalike performance.
  - When to use: After launch, to judge effectiveness and optimize future campaigns.
  - How: Track (1) Reach & Impressions, (2) Click-Through Rate (CTR), (3) Conversion Rate, (4) Return on Investment (ROI = revenue from campaign ÷ campaign cost; positive ROI = profitable).

## Key Concepts
- **Lookalike Audience**: New people Meta finds who resemble your existing customers in demographics, interests, and behaviors.
- **Seed Audience**: The source group of already-interested people Meta uses as the template to find similar users.
- **Google Search Audience Feed**: A list of people who recently searched Google for your keyword; the author's preferred initial seed data source (introduced in Chapter 2) because it captures high-intent users.
- **Audience Manager**: Meta's tool where you upload a past-customer list (whole list or a segment) to build a lookalike from it.
- **CPM (Cost per 1,000 Impressions)**: The metric lookalikes lower by widening targeting, which in turn lowers Cost per Acquisition.
- **Audience Size**: The chosen breadth of the lookalike (1–100% of seed), trading similarity for reach.
- **ROI**: Revenue generated by the lookalike campaign divided by its cost; the financial verdict on the audience.

## Mental Models
- **Think of lookalikes as "finding your customers' doppelgängers across the entire internet."** Meta is a trained scout that does the discovery work for you — you don't need to know who the new people are.
- **Treat your seed like the A-list guests at a party.** Seed from your *best* customers (repeat buyers, raving fans) rather than all customers, because a sharper, higher-quality seed produces a stronger lookalike.
- **Use small (1–10%) lookalikes when precision matters; large (51–100%) when reach matters.** Similarity and scale are inversely related — pick the tier that fits the goal.
- **Use lookalikes as a cost lever, not just a reach lever.** Wider targeting → lower CPM → lower CPA, so deploy them "extensively" to reduce overall costs.

## Anti-patterns
- **Blindly casting a wide net and hoping**: The pre-lookalike default; wastes spend on people who don't care. Lookalikes replace guesswork with data-driven, tailored targeting.
- **Seeding from an undersized audience**: Too few people gives Meta insufficient data for an accurate lookalike; use at least ~1,000 people in the seed.
- **Seeding from an irrelevant or unengaged list**: Seed members must resemble your real target market and have engaged (visited site, liked page, purchased); a weak seed yields a weak lookalike.
- **Including already-converted users in the lookalike**: Exclude people who have already converted so spend focuses on net-new prospects.
- **Letting a poorly-performing audience run unchanged**: If it underperforms, adjust the size or switch to a different seed rather than persisting.

## Key Takeaways
1. Seed quality drives lookalike quality — prioritize **Size (≥1,000), Relevance, and Engagement** in your seed audience.
2. Prefer high-intent seed data; the author's top pick is the **Google Search Audience Feed** (from Chapter 2), then uploaded customer lists via Audience Manager.
3. Match audience size to goal: small % for precision/similarity, large % for reach.
4. Lookalikes lower CPM, which lowers CPA — use them extensively as a cost-reduction tool, not only for expansion.
5. Tailor ad creative, copy, and placements to the lookalike's interests; a good audience still needs relevant ads.
6. Always measure reach, impressions, CTR, conversion rate, and ROI after launch, then optimize.
7. Optimize by adjusting size, excluding already-converted users, or swapping the seed.
