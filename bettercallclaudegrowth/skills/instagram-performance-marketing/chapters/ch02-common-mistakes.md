# Chapter 2: Common Mistakes That Are Killing Your Ads Conversion Rate

## Core Idea
Most poor ad performance comes from a handful of repeatable setup mistakes — over-trusting Meta's algorithm, targeting the wrong geos and audiences, and starving campaigns of conversion data and spend. Fix these and you drive your Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) to the lowest possible level.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The 9 Common Mistakes (conversion-killer checklist)**: a diagnostic list of recurring errors the author sees across agencies and brands when running ads at volume.
  - When to use: audit any underperforming or expensive Meta/Instagram conversion campaign before scaling spend.
  - How (the nine):
    1. **Trusting Facebook's Algorithm** — running top-of-funnel ads with no interest targeting and hoping the algorithm finds your audience.
    2. **US-Only Geo Targeting** — restricting delivery to the US only.
    3. **Low Intent Audiences** — not targeting high-intent Google Search Audiences.
    4. **Utilizing the Wrong Creative Type** — relying on static ads instead of video.
    5. **Selecting the Wrong Ad Settings** — misconfiguring campaign options that drive cost and performance.
    6. **Selecting the Wrong Conversion Objective** — picking an objective that mismatches your business goal.
    7. **Lack of Conversion Input** — not feeding existing customer data to the algorithm.
    8. **Not Front Loading Ad Spend** — spending too little per day to exit the learning phase.
    9. **iFrame Issues** — the in-app browser (iframe) window breaking your landing/sign-up forms.

## Key Concepts
- **Learning Phase**: the period during which Meta's algorithm needs ~50 conversion events to stabilize delivery; reaching it is expensive if mishandled.
- **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**: the cost to acquire one converting customer; the primary metric this chapter optimizes downward.
- **CPM (Cost per 1,000 Impressions)**: cost to reach 1,000 people; US-only targeting can push it to $150–$350, while adding other proven-converting countries can drop it below $1.
- **High Intent Audience**: consumers recently searching Google for your keywords — acquired as a Google Search Audience and uploaded to Facebook Audience Manager.
- **Look-a-like Audience**: an audience Meta builds from your seed data (customer list or Google Search Audience) by matching similar traits and behaviors.
- **Re-Engagement / Retargeting Audience by Video Watch Time**: retargeting pools built from how long viewers watched your video ads — only available if you run video creative.
- **UGC (User Generated Content) video ads**: native-feeling video creative (can be made with Generative AI) that can lift CTR up to 10%.
- **CTR (Click Through Rate)**: share of impressions that click; lifted by UGC video creative.
- **Conversion Objective**: the campaign goal you select; it determines which account types Meta targets and has the largest single impact on performance.
- **Pixel**: tracking mechanism for logging new customer conversions to feed the algorithm.

## Mental Models
- **Think of the algorithm as a tool you feed, not an oracle you trust.** Don't let Facebook "figure out your audience" — give it interest targeting, customer lists, and pixel data so it converges fast and cheap.
- **Treat geography as a CPM lever, not a default.** If location doesn't matter to your offer, every US-only impression is "lighting money on fire"; broaden to proven-converting countries to slash CPM.
- **Use spend velocity, not spend size, to escape the learning phase.** Concentrate the majority of your two-week budget early (after confirming initial low-cost conversions) so Meta gathers conversion data quickly.
- **Prefer video over static because video builds assets.** Static may convert, but video creates watch-time-based retargeting pools you can re-engage later.

## Anti-patterns
- **Top-of-funnel ads with zero interest targeting**: reaching 50 conversion events (the learning phase) becomes very expensive, and post-learning CPA stays far higher than necessary.
- **US-only geo targeting**: inflates CPM to $150–$350 and wastes budget reaching far fewer people.
- **Ignoring high-intent Google searchers**: skips the most conversion-ready audience you could be retargeting on Meta.
- **Static-only creative**: forfeits video-watch-time retargeting and the CTR upside of UGC video.
- **Not seeding from your own customers**: with 1,000+ past purchasers, failing to build a look-a-like means overpaying for every new acquisition.
- **Trickle spending (a few dollars/day)**: never exits the learning phase, so CPA never drops and Meta never gets enough conversion data.
- **Unchecked iframe behavior**: broken login/sign-up forms inside the Facebook/Instagram in-app browser silently kill conversions and ad spend.

## Key Takeaways
1. Don't outsource audience discovery to the algorithm — add interest targeting and seed data to cut CPA.
2. Drop CPM by targeting beyond the US when location is irrelevant to the offer; this has the biggest single CPM effect.
3. Acquire high-intent Google Search Audiences, upload them to Facebook Audience Manager, and build look-a-likes from them.
4. Run video (ideally AI-generated UGC) to unlock watch-time retargeting and lift CTR up to 10%.
5. Match your conversion objective to your business — it has the largest impact on performance.
6. Upload existing customer lists (1,000+) and fire pixels on new conversions to build cheaper, more reliable look-a-like audiences.
7. Front-load your two-week budget once ads convert at low cost, to escape the learning phase fast.
8. Test that your landing, login, and sign-up forms actually work inside the Facebook/Instagram iframe.
