# Chapter 6: GEO Targeting

## Core Idea
Geo targeting is the primary lever on CPM, and the fastest way to lower CPM (and therefore CPA) is to *expand* your geo targeting into additional countries — especially low-competition emerging markets — that still convert at the end of your funnel. Counterintuitively, broadening reach narrows cost.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Meta's Geo-Targeting Toolbox (4 options)**: The author's breakdown of Meta's targeting controls, ordered from precise to broad.
  - When to use: Anytime you build a campaign and must decide *where* impressions are served.
  - How — four mechanisms:
    1. **Location-Based Targeting** — choose intent of presence: *People living in this location* (residency matters, e.g. local real estate), *People recently in this location* (transient crowds, e.g. airport/tourist spots), *People traveling in this location* (souvenirs, travel apps).
    2. **Targeting by Radius** — draw a circle around a point, from 1 mile to 50 miles; best for local businesses and events where people must physically show up.
    3. **Regional Targeting** — Meta's pre-grouped country bundles: *North America* (US, Canada, Mexico), *European Union* (GDPR-compliant, "sophisticated" goods), *Emerging Markets* (Southeast Asia, Africa, Latin America — high engagement, low CPM).
    4. **Custom Location Groups** — build your own country/city set from market research (e.g. winter jackets → Canada + Norway + Siberia; beach towels → Australia + Brazil + California).

- **Broad vs. Specific Targeting decision rule**: Match targeting width to campaign objective rather than always hyper-targeting.
  - When to go **broad**: testing a new product or audience; entering emerging markets; awareness campaigns where reach trumps precision.
  - When to go **specific**: retargeting warm audiences; promoting local events/businesses; selling niche products with a narrow appeal.

## Key Concepts
- **CPM (cost per thousand impressions)**: The auction price for 1,000 ad impressions; the dominant driver of CPA in this chapter's logic.
- **Geo expansion for CPM reduction**: Adding many converting countries to a campaign to dilute auction competition and drop CPM (author cites lowering a client's CPM to under $1 while still converting).
- **Emerging markets**: Lower-competition, rapidly-growing, high-engagement regions (India, Indonesia, Nigeria) where CPMs are a fraction of US/EU rates.
- **Relevance score**: Meta's reward signal — more relevant ads earn lower CPMs.
- **Optimized delivery**: Giving Meta's algorithm a smaller, more qualified audience so it can deliver more efficiently.
- **Localization**: Adapting language, currency, and imagery per market so one creative is not blindly reused across countries.

## Mental Models
- **Geo-targeting as carnival placement**: Selling snow cones next to cotton candy gets crickets; move to the bumper cars where everyone's sweating and you're king of crushed ice. Put ads where customers are primed to convert.
- **The narrow-broad paradox**: Geo targeting narrows focus *and* broadens reach at the same time — strategic location choice both lowers CPM and stretches budget.
- **Think of US/Canada CPM as creative tax**: With CPMs as high as ~$300 in US/Canadian regions, your creative must "work overtime" to win converting impressions; cheaper geos relieve that pressure.

## Anti-patterns
- **Running one English-language ad across many countries**: The "rookie mistake" — skipping localization (language, currency, imagery) kills resonance in non-English markets.
- **Defaulting to hyper-targeting**: Going too narrow drives high CPMs and limited reach.
- **Casting too wide indiscriminately**: An over-broad net dilutes relevance and wastes spend on people who won't convert.
- **Ignoring CPM when CPA is high**: If ads aren't hitting a low enough CPA, the cause is usually an unaddressed CPM problem, often fixable via geo.

## Why Geo-Targeting Lowers CPM (mechanism)
1. **Less competition**: Fewer advertisers per region means lower auction prices.
2. **Higher relevance scores**: Meta rewards audience-relevant ads with lower CPMs.
3. **Optimized delivery**: A smaller, more qualified audience lets the algorithm perform better.

## Key Takeaways
1. Treat CPM as the root cause of a poor CPA — fix geo before blaming creative.
2. To cut CPM fast, *add* more converting countries; don't just tighten one market.
3. Mine emerging markets (Southeast Asia, Africa, Latin America) for low CPM + high engagement, but validate they still convert through your funnel.
4. Pick the targeting mechanism by intent: living/recently/traveling, radius, region, or custom group.
5. Localize every expanded market — language, currency, imagery — instead of cloning the US ad.
6. Go broad for testing/awareness/new markets; go specific for retargeting, local events, and niche products.
