# Chapter 1: GEO Fundamentals — Transferred Trust, Zero-Click, and BoFu Pivot

## Core Idea
SEO does not die — it splits in two: alongside "being found" on Google, a new goal emerges: "being cited as a source" inside AI-generated answers (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity). The collapse of the click on informational queries forces a shift in editorial investment from ToFu to BoFu.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Trust transfer (distributed social proof)**: a recommendation from an LLM is processed as advice, not advertising. Appearing as the first choice in an AI response is an editorial position (like the NYT), not an advertising one (like a Google Ad). You don't pay the engine for it — you pay in editorial work.
  - When to use it: to justify the GEO business case for B2B / high-consideration products.
- **ToFu→BoFu Pivot**: redistribute the editorial budget away from informational content (what it is, how it works) toward Bottom-of-Funnel content that LLMs scan to recommend products.
  - How: produce the 4 high-yield BoFu formats (below).
- **The 4 high-yield BoFu formats for 2026**:
  1. **Vertical listicles** — "The best 7 tools for [job]". The most parseable format for "what is the best X".
  2. **Direct comparisons** — "X vs Y". The quintessential commercial query; the most cited in commercial AI Overviews.
  3. **Buyer's guides** — with an explicit decision matrix (criteria, tables, use cases).
  4. **Alternatives pages** — "[Competitor] alternatives". Explicit intent, high conversion.

## Key Concepts
- **GEO**: Generative Engine Optimization — visibility in generative engines and AI interfaces.
- **Zero-click search**: a search resolved within the interface without a click (AI Overview, Knowledge Panel, Featured Snippet).
- **BoFu**: Bottom-of-Funnel, content for users ready to purchase.
- **AI Visibility Prompt / Prompt-target**: the conversational question under which you want to be cited (≠ Google keyword).

## Mental Models
- Think of the generative model as **a journalist doing fact-checking**: give it verifiable facts, primary sources, declared expertise, and a readable structure.
- "Either you're in the AI response, or you're beyond position 5" — in that case you can no longer recover the traffic.
- Verify the "round number" claims circulating in the GEO community: the direction is right, but the proportions need re-validation before budget decisions.

## Anti-patterns
- **Believing the "17× conversion" claim**: the verified figure is 4.4× average (Semrush), 9-11× on ChatGPT in research-heavy verticals; in pure transactional e-commerce it drops to +31% or vanishes entirely.
- **Investing in GEO for high-volume e-commerce on short queries**: the old SEO/SEM playbook remains more efficient. GEO pays off for SaaS, B2B, consulting, education, and complex products.
- **Abandoning informational SEO entirely**: topical authority (hubs), long-tail with latent commercial intent, and content with proprietary data all remain alive and valuable.

## Key Takeaways
1. SEO and GEO are two parallel programs with partly distinct levers (see ch04).
2. First strategic filter: GEO pays off if the product requires an informed decision.
3. The BoFu format wins because it is what LLMs read to make recommendations.
4. Heading-as-question + concise 1-2-line answer below = the pattern LLMs extract.
5. Originality with proprietary data is rewarded by both Google (Core Update Mar 2026) and LLMs.
