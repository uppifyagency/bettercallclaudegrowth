# Patterns — Productizing Yourself

Concrete, reusable techniques from the book. Run them roughly in order — each builds on the prior.

## The Uniqueness Audit
**When to use**: Before niching down or building any product.
**How**: List every task in your current workflow → mark the ones you're *good at* AND *enjoy* → pick the single highest-leverage one. (Jack: pitch decks / idea distillation.)
**Trade-offs**: Forces you to drop services you *can* do but shouldn't — short-term revenue dip for long-term focus.

## Deliberate Practice Loop
**When to use**: Step 1, while honing the underlying skill.
**How**: (1) Set a specific goal; (2) do purposeful (non-habitual) reps; (3) target specific weaknesses; (4) build a system to get and apply feedback. Count *iterations*, not hours.
**Trade-offs**: Slower and more uncomfortable than mindless practice; far higher skill gain per rep.

## Publish-as-You-Learn (Proof of Work)
**When to use**: Continuously, from day one.
**How**: Set a fixed publishing routine (e.g., 1 hr/week), keep a content calendar, decide topics in advance (kills writer's block), and **engage with everyone who engages with you**.
**Trade-offs**: Early audience is tiny ("only my mom commented"); compounds only with consistency over years.

## Systematize the Repeatable (Step 2 toolkit)
**When to use**: As soon as clients start requesting your service repeatedly.
**How**: Templatize emails (onboarding/feedback/follow-up); document your process (saves time + markets professionalism); create intake forms; niche your copy so clients self-select.
**Trade-offs**: Upfront setup time; some flexibility lost — but you "lose your job" to systems, which is the goal.

## Productize (Step 3 menu)
**When to use**: Only after reps + systems are real.
**How**: Pick the lightest format that solves the problem without you — short self-published book, course (studio *or* voiceover-on-slides), template (hand over the framework), or software/automation.
**Trade-offs**: Build-once cost up front; income decouples from your hours afterward.

## The Brand Iceberg
**When to use**: Whenever you feel pressure to "build a personal brand."
**How**: Stop performing. Pursue genuine curiosity (the mass below water) and publish a fraction of what you learn (the tip above). Brand = curiosity × time spent on it.
**Trade-offs**: Requires real depth and patience; cannot be faked or rushed.

## Deep Assets + Front-Door Content
**When to use**: When scaling distribution of products.
**How**: Build deep assets (curriculums, products) first; then post short content that links/points to them. Volume of short content rises *because* you have depth to point to.
**Trade-offs**: Surface content alone goes nowhere — you must invest in the assets first.

## Discover-Your-Niche Prompts
**When to use**: Choosing what brand/topic to build around.
**How**: (1) Ask close friends what you'd be great at writing about; (2) brain-dump interests on paper, circle the 1–3 with most energy; (3) review sent emails for questions you keep answering. Pivot later if needed.
**Trade-offs**: Picking a lane feels limiting but is what makes you findable.

## Permissionless Mentorship
**When to use**: Skill-building stage, especially without formal education.
**How**: Identify the skill → find masters who publish freely → follow, study, and apply their best work. Pair with a community learning the same skill (Skillshare, Udemy).
**Trade-offs**: No direct access/feedback from the mentor — you compensate with community.

## Feedback-Driven Iteration Ladder
**When to use**: Evolving a service toward a product.
**How**: Skill → service → systematized service (forms/videos as leverage) → product. Let customer feedback decide what to systematize and what to productize at each rung.
**Trade-offs**: Slower than launching a product cold, but each rung de-risks the next.
