# Glossary — Productize Yourself

**Advice is abundant, execution is scarce** — Jack's closing principle: knowing is common, doing is rare and valuable (Ch 4).

**Brand Iceberg** — model where your visible brand is the small tip above water, supported by a hidden mass of curiosity, reading, and thinking below (Ch 3).

**Build in public** — publishing your learning and creating process openly, not just finished results (Ch 3).

**Deliberate Practice** — purposeful, feedback-driven repetition targeting specific weaknesses; not mindless habit (Ch 2). Cited via *Outliers*, *Deep Work*, *Peak*.

**Exponential reach** — the internet's ability to spread/scale things without proportional added effort; the opposite of linear service work (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Get going, then get good** — Jack's principle: start before you're ready; quality comes from volume + feedback in public (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Leverage (onboarding assets)** — forms, videos, and docs that do repetitive explaining once, freeing your time (Ch 2, Ch 3).

**Linear income** — revenue bound to hours worked; one project = one paycheck; stop working = stop earning (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**"Lose your job"** — deliberately replace yourself with systems and tools so the business doesn't depend on your manual work (Ch 2).

**Opponent** — Jack Butcher's advertising agency, launched 2017; successful but stuck in the time trap (Ch 1).

**Permissionless mentors** — masters who publish their best work for free; you can learn from them without their knowledge or permission (Ch 3).

**Proof of work** — published output (writing, design, code, tweets) that functions as a credential, replacing résumés (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Productize yourself** — turn a personal skill into a scalable, sellable product or asset that earns without your direct involvement (all chapters).

**Put in the reps** — Step 1 of the method: do the unscalable work to hone your skill and build reputation (Ch 2).

**Seek wealth, not money or status** — Naval Ravikant principle: wealth = assets that earn while you sleep; own equity rather than rent out time (Ch 1).

**Self-selection** — niching your marketing copy so good-fit clients qualify themselves in and poor-fit ones out (Ch 2).

**Solve problems at scale** — Step 3: turn skill + systems into products (books, courses, templates, software) that sell 24/7 (Ch 2).

**Ten thousand iterations (not hours)** — Jack's twist on Gladwell: mastery comes from feedback-driven iterations, not time served (Ch 2).

**The time trap** — the service-business ceiling where income is capped by available working hours (Ch 1).

**Tweets as front doors** — short content that points attention toward deeper assets (curriculums, products) (Ch 3).

**Uniqueness Question** — *"What am I uniquely good at in this entire process?"* — the audit that reveals your productization seed (Ch 1).

**Visualize Value** — Jack Butcher's brand of minimalist black-and-white graphics distilling complex ideas; launched 2019, $1M+/year (Ch 1).

**Vet clients with copy** — Step 2 tactic: narrow the offer so marketing copy itself filters inquiries (Ch 2).
