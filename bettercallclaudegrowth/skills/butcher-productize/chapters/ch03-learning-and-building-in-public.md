# Chapter 3: Learning and Building in Public

## Core Idea
The other half of productizing yourself is building an online brand — not by performing or pretending, but by sharing your genuine curiosity consistently over years. The audience is what makes the products sell.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Brand Iceberg** — Jack's central model for personal brand.
  - *Below the surface* (unseen): your curiosity, reading, thinking, idea-processing — the deep foundation.
  - *Above the surface* (your brand): the small fraction you publish, which only has depth *because* of the foundation below.
  - When to use: Whenever you "panic" about building a personal brand.
  - How: Stop trying to manufacture a persona; instead pursue real curiosity and share what you're learning. *"The sum of your curiosity, and the time you spent pursuing your curiosity, is your brand."* (Jack Butcher)

- **Tweets as front doors to deep assets** — short content points attention toward your developed assets (curriculums, products). Volume of posts went up *because* Jack had deep assets to point to. Surface content without depth has nothing to lead to.

- **Permissionless mentors** — learn from masters who publish their best work for free; they never need to know you're studying them. The internet replaces the university.
  - How: Pick the skill → find the people who've mastered it and publish → follow, study, apply.

- **Feedback-and-iteration loop** — the Visualize Value business model itself was *discovered* by iterating: skill → service → systematized service → product. Customer feedback drove each step.

## Key Concepts
- **Build in public** — publish the learning process, not just finished wins.
- **Niche / online avatar** — become the avatar for one specific idea/industry rather than a generalist with opinions on everything (you can pivot later).
- **Consistency over time** — Jack published from 2010 ("only my mom commented") for years before takeoff.
- **Community** — it's very hard to build a skill in solitude; learn alongside others chasing the same skill (Skillshare, Udemy as starting points).

## Mental Models
- **Brand = curiosity × time spent pursuing it.** Authenticity beats performance.
- **You can't shortcut a brand any more than you can shortcut mastery** — both require real depth.
- **Double down on what works** — note which posts succeed, do more of that; experiment across forms (writing, graphics, photos, video) and platforms (Twitter, LinkedIn, Reddit, YouTube, email) until one resonates.

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating a personal brand as a performance** — putting on a show or pretending to be someone you aren't "misses the mark."
- **Generalist with an opinion on everything** — hard to become known; pick a lane.
- **Publishing surface content with no underlying assets** — front doors that open onto empty rooms.
- **Expecting overnight results** — quitting before consistency compounds.
- **Leaving engagers hanging** — not responding to people who react to your work.

## Key Takeaways
1. Your brand is the visible tip of an iceberg of genuine curiosity — feed the part below the water.
2. Build deep assets first; use short content as front doors to them.
3. Find a niche and become the avatar for one idea (pivot allowed).
4. Learn from permissionless mentors and within a community — skill rarely grows in solitude.
5. Consistency + experimentation over years is the actual mechanism; Jack published a decade before takeoff.
