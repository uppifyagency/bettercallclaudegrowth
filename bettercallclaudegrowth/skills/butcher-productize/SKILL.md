## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The master thesis: linear → exponential
Service work scales **linearly** — one project = one paycheck; stop working, stop earning; income capped by hours in a day (the **time trap**). The internet is built for **exponential** spread. Productizing yourself = moving income off the linear curve onto the exponential one by turning a skill into assets that sell without your direct involvement. *Seek wealth (assets that earn while you sleep), not money or status* — own equity, don't rent time (Naval Ravikant, the thread that turned Jack around).

### The 3-Step Productization Method (the spine of the book — non-skippable, in order)
1. **Put in the reps** — hone the skill *and* publish; do the unscalable work (apply to 150 jobs, post 100 illustrations). Aim for **10,000 iterations, not 10,000 hours** — feedback-driven reps, not time served. Principle: **"Get going, then get good."** Your published output is **proof of work** — it beats a résumé.
2. **Discover repeating processes** — systematize what you do for every client so you **"lose your job"** to systems: email templates, process documentation, intake forms, and niched marketing copy that makes clients **self-select**. *"It's not about never doing things that don't scale; it's about not repeating yourself when you don't have to."*
3. **Solve problems at scale** — turn skill + systems into products that sell 24/7: a short self-published **book**, a **course**, **templates** (hand over the framework), or **software/automation**. You can't reach Step 3 without Steps 1–2.

### The Uniqueness Question
*"What am I uniquely good at in this entire process?"* — audit your workflow, find the one task you're both **good at AND enjoy** (Jack: distilling ideas into punchy decks), niche to it, drop the rest. That's your productization seed.

### The Brand Iceberg
Your visible brand is the small tip above water; below it sits the hidden mass of curiosity, reading, and thinking. *"The sum of your curiosity, and the time you spent pursuing it, is your brand."* Don't perform a persona — pursue genuine curiosity and publish a fraction of what you learn. Build **deep assets** first; use short content (tweets) as **front doors** to them. Brand, like mastery, **cannot be shortcut**.

### Deliberate Practice (how to actually improve)
Specific goal → purposeful (non-habitual) reps → target specific weaknesses → a system to get and apply feedback. Mindless repetition doesn't count.

### Permissionless mentors + community
Learn from masters who publish their best work for free (they never need to know). The internet replaces the university — but skill rarely grows in solitude, so learn inside a community chasing the same skill.

### The closing principle
**"Advice is abundant, execution is scarce."** The "overnight success" is a decade of invisible reps + consistent publishing + one decision to *solve problems that scale*. No shortcuts.
