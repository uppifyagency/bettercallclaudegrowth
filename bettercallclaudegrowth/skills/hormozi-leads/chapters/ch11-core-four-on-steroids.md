# Chapter 11: Core Four on Steroids: More, Better, New + The Rule of 100

## Core Idea
Once any of the Core Four works at all, you scale it the same three ways every time — do MORE of it, do it BETTER, or do it somewhere NEW — and the fastest lever is almost always MORE, executed via the Rule of 100. There are vastly more leads available than any business assumes; plateaus are an advertising-skill problem, not a market-size problem.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **More, Better, New**: The three (and only three) ways to boost any Core Four activity for more engaged leads. (1) Do MORE of what's working, (2) do it BETTER, (3) do it somewhere NEW.
  - When to use: Whenever you're already running a Core Four method but want more engaged lead flow.
  - How: Exhaust More and Better first (they're cheaper and faster), then expand to New. "What's stopping them from doing 10x what they're currently doing?" — answer that and you know whether you need More or Better next.

- **The Rule of 100**: How you do MORE. Advertise by doing 100 primary actions every day, for 100 days straight. The book's one promise: do this and you will get more engaged leads.
  - When to use: First, before optimizing anything. Crank volume to max capacity; even with zero improvements, doubling inputs gets more leads.
  - How (per Core Four):
    - Warm Reach Outs: 100 reach outs/day (email, text, DM, calls)
    - Post Content: 100 minutes/day making content, release at least one piece/day
    - Cold Reach Outs: 100 reach outs/day (email, text, DM, cold call, flyers) — use automation, expect lower response
    - Paid Ads: 100 minutes/day making ads, 100 days straight running them at your calculated daily budget; aim for Client-Financed Acquisition

- **Testing for BETTER — one test per week per platform**: Better = more leads for the same effort, achieved only through testing. Run exactly one split test per platform per week.
  - When to use: When you've pushed More until it "breaks" (CAC gets too high to sustain).
  - How: Every Monday launch one split test per platform; the next Monday (1) pick winners, (2) log results so you build on past wins, (3) design the next test to beat your current best. If you can't beat the current version in 4 tries (one month), move to the next constraint.

- **Constraints (biggest drop-off step)**: The step where the most leads drop off. Small improvements there create the biggest boost, so do the most testing on the constraint.
  - When to use: To decide WHAT to test next.
  - How: Map each step's conversion %; the step with the largest drop-off is the constraint. A 5-point lift on a 5% step (→10%) is a 2x increase in leads, far more than the same lift on a 30% or 50% step.

- **The Size Of The Pie Fallacy**: Mistaking the tiny slice you currently advertise to (one Core Four, one platform, one way, one targeted audience, a handful of visible competitors) for the entire available market. This is why most businesses stay small — "I'm as big as I can get" feels easier than "I'm not as good at advertising as I thought."
  - When to use: As the rationale for going NEW; recognize it whenever you feel "saturated."

## Key Concepts
- **More**: Do a lot more of what already works — double reach outs, content, ads, ad spend. The biggest increases often come from here.
- **Better**: Same effort, more leads, via thousands of tiny tests; "do more until it breaks, then make it better."
- **New**: New ways (content styles) or new places (platforms), then a whole new Core Four activity, once More/Better returns drop.
- **Primary action**: The single repeatable advertising action you count toward 100/day (a reach out, a piece of content, an ad).
- **Constraint**: The highest-drop-off step; the smallest improvement here yields the biggest result.
- **Test log**: A written record of all tests so each new test starts "a zillion improvements later, not at square one."
- **Order for "New"**: New placement → New platform → New Core Four — chosen by what gets the most leads for the least work.
- **Throughput**: Overall conversion rate from click to close; if untracked, you can't know whether you're saturated or just unmeasured.

## Mental Models
- **Use "More until it breaks, then Better" when scaling**: Push volume until CAC becomes unsustainable; the next drop-off point becomes obvious, then go fix the constraint. More and Better work together, not separately.
- **Think of the constraint as the cheapest leverage you own**: Fixing a 5% step beats polishing a 50% step many times over. Spend your one weekly test where leads bleed most.
- **Use "New" only at diminishing returns**: When the effort put into More/Better yields less than the same effort on a new placement, platform, or Core Four, switch. Not before.
- **Think of the Core Four as compounding, not competing**: Money, systems, and experience from one method make the next one work better. Content + ads beats either alone.

## Anti-patterns
- **Assuming you've saturated the market**: A $2M business spending $30k/mo on one platform in a $15.1B niche has not saturated anything; it's untested and under-advertised.
- **Not tracking throughput**: If you don't know your click-to-close rate, you can't tell whether you're maxed out or just blind.
- **Jumping to New before exhausting More/Better**: New is harder in practice; skipping the cheap levers wastes the highest-ROI effort.
- **Testing multiple things at once**: Change several steps and you never learn what actually worked — and steps affect each other.
- **Wasting your one weekly test on trivia**: A red-to-bright-red color change burns your scarce test slot; spend it on the constraint.

## Key Takeaways
1. Commit to the Rule of 100 — 100 primary actions/day for 100 days — before optimizing anything; volume is the fastest lever.
2. Scale every Core Four method with More → Better → New, in that order; exhaust the cheap levers first.
3. Track throughput; you can't claim saturation if you've never measured your click-to-close rate.
4. Find the constraint (biggest drop-off step) and aim your testing there for the largest lift per unit of effort.
5. Run exactly one split test per platform per week, log every result, and move to the next constraint if you can't beat your best in four tries.
6. When returns flatten, go New in this order: new placement, new platform, new Core Four activity — picking by most leads per unit of work.
7. The Core Four compound: every combination boosts the others, so master one fully before stacking the next.
