# Chapter 7: Post Free Content II: Monetize, Scale & Free Goodwill

## Core Idea
Monetize a warm audience by over-giving until people ask to buy, then make clean "asks" without killing the goodwill that grew the audience — and treat free content as a demand-nurturer that lifts the returns of every other advertising method.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Give-Give-Give-Ask (give until they ask)**: Deposit goodwill repeatedly with rewarding content, then occasionally withdraw it with an ask. Hormozi's upgrade is to dramatically over-give until the audience asks *you* for the sale.
  - When to use: Always, but especially when your goal is to grow the audience (not just squeeze it).
  - How: Give in public, ask in private — let the audience self-select when they're ready to pay. The more patient you are, the more you get when you finally ask. Growth never slows because you never trigger the "now they're selling me" reflex.

- **The Give:Ask Ratio**: Track how much you give relative to how much you ask. Mature, monetizing platforms run ~3.5:1 (TV: 47 min give / 13 min ask per hour) or ~4:1 (Facebook: 4 posts per 1 ad). That's the floor. Growing platforms over-give far beyond it.
  - When to use: To audit whether your asks are too frequent before they shrink your audience.
  - How: Model growing platforms, not mature ones. Stay well above 3:1. "Give, give, give, ask" is the maximum-monetization-without-shrinking ratio — but to grow, give far more.

- **Two Ask Strategies (Integrated vs. Intermittent)**:
  - **Integrated**: Put the ask *inside* every content piece while keeping the give:ask ratio high (e.g., 3×30s ads in a 60-min podcast = 58.5 min give / 1.5 min ask). Best for long-form platforms.
  - **Intermittent**: Make many pure "give" pieces, then one dedicated "ask" piece (e.g., 10 gives, ask on the 11th). Best for short-form platforms.
  - How: Start by placing one CTA after a valuable moment or at the end; confirm growth doesn't slow; then add more. After every ask, get back to giving.

- **Two Scaling Strategies (Depth-then-Width vs. Width-then-Depth)**:
  - **Depth then Width**: Post → post regularly → maximize quality + quantity on that one platform (short form up to ~10×/day; long form up to ~5 days/week) → add the next platform → repeat. Compounds faster, fewer resources, but risks single-channel dependence.
  - **Width then Depth**: Get on every relevant platform early (repurpose one piece across all), then maximize them all at once. Broader reach faster, but more labor and a risk of "sucky fluff" everywhere.
  - When to use: Depth-then-width when resources are tight; width-then-depth when you already have a sizable business.

## Key Concepts
- **The Ask = how content makes money**: Money-wise the ask is "free"; its real cost is potential lost trust, slowed growth, and the time spent building the audience.
- **Two things to advertise in any ask**: Either your **core offer** (go straight for the sale) or your **Lead Magnet** (lower risk — if unsure, use this). Don't overcomplicate.
- **Lead Magnet ask flow**: Tease extra value ("I have 11 more tips — grab them on my site"), then the thank-you page after opt-in shows your paid offer.
- **Asks as commercials**: You interrupt your own program with a "very important message" — but you pay the cost since it's your audience.
- **Don't kill the golden goose**: Over-asking can make a growing channel shrink (a real friend's podcast did exactly this).
- **Free content as demand-nurturer**: 78% of clients consumed at least TWO long-form pieces before booking a call — content warms people even when it isn't your primary method.
- **#mozination / Free Goodwill**: The book practices what it preaches by making an ask of the reader (leave a review) framed as helping a faceless fellow entrepreneur at zero cost.

## Mental Models
- **Use "give until they ask" when you want compounding growth**: The best customers are the biggest "givers" — people who feel they got the better end of the deal even after paying. Discomfort at receiving without reciprocating drives them to come to you.
- **Think of measuring outputs as only useful if inputs are constant**: Pick a posting cadence and an ask cadence you'll stick with, then "Do. Not. Stop." You only control inputs.
- **Think of attention as standards, not span**: "There's no such thing as too long, only too boring." People binge long-form they like; circumstances changed, biology didn't.
- **Use "How I" instead of "How to"**: Share experience ("my favorite way") not prescriptions ("the best way"). When you speak from experience, no one can question you — it's bulletproof and avoids sounding preachy.

## Anti-patterns
- **Asking too frequently / too soon**: Eager monetization of a new audience can make it stop growing and shrink. The moment you start asking is the moment you decide to slow growth.
- **Giving paid ads all the credit**: Hormozi stopped making relevant (gym) content, paid ads declined, and he blamed the ads — free content was silently nurturing demand.
- **Making general content as a small/local player**: The audience will follow people with better track records. Stay in your "puddle" first (Puddles → Ponds → Lakes → Oceans).
- **Pre-scheduling posts**: Scheduled posts perform worse — the tight feedback loop of manually pressing "submit" makes you try harder.
- **Treating free content as low-stakes**: Bad free content makes paying customers value your paid product less; good free content retains them and boosts their perceived ROI.
- **Waiting to start**: "The best day to start posting was the day you were born. The second best is today. Don't wait like I did."

## Key Takeaways
1. Over-give relentlessly and ask in private — "give until they ask" produces the best customers and growth that never slows.
2. Keep your give:ask ratio well above 3:1; never let monetization shrink your audience.
3. When you ask, advertise either your core offer or your Lead Magnet (default to the Lead Magnet — lower risk), then immediately return to giving.
4. Choose Integrated asks for long-form and Intermittent asks for short-form.
5. Make content even if it's not your primary method — 78% of clients consumed 2+ long-form pieces before booking; content lifts returns on every other channel.
6. Measure both absolute and relative growth monthly, stay consistent on inputs, and give time, time (Hormozi's podcast took 5 years to reach Top 10).
7. Make your first post today; switch to "How I," remind more than you teach, and start narrow ("king of that puddle").
