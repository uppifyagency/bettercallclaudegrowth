# Chapter 1: Start Here: The Game, The Promise & The Business Model

## Core Idea
Advertising is a learnable skill, and leads are the lifeblood of every business — once you can reliably generate leads, you get endless second chances and it becomes "hard to be poor." This book's entire promise is one thing: leads, lots of leads.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Money Equation (Offer → Leads → Sales → Money)**: You can't skip to "make money." You need all the pieces: an offer (the stuff to sell), leads (people to sell it to), and sales (getting them to buy). $100M Offers covered the offer; $100M Leads covers the leads.
  - When to use: As the orienting mental map before doing any advertising work.
  - How: Confirm you have a **Grand Slam Offer** first (this book assumes it). Then solve "who do I sell it to?" → leads.
- **The Two Ways to Grow a Business**: You can only grow in two ways — (1) Get more customers, or (2) Make them worth more. $100M Leads focuses entirely on #1.
  - When to use: Deciding where to invest growth effort.
  - How: This book attacks "get more customers" by improving four lead dimensions below.
- **The Four Lead Goals (More / Better / Cheaper / Reliably)**: Get more customers by getting (1) More leads, (2) Better leads, (3) Cheaper leads, (4) Reliably — from lots of places.
  - When to use: As the scorecard for every advertising activity.
  - How: Audit each channel against all four; "from lots of places" is the reliability hedge against any single source dying.
- **Hormozi's Business Model (4 steps)**: (1) Provide better free products than the marketplace's paid products; (2) Earn the trust of entrepreneurs making over $1M/year in profit; (3) Invest in those entrepreneurs to fast-track growth; (4) Help everyone else for free, for good.
  - When to use: Understanding why the book is free/at-cost and what Acquisition.com actually does.
  - How: The book IS the trust-building free product; Acquisition.com buys equity in profitable, bootstrapped companies and accelerates them with shared systems.

## Key Concepts
- **Lead**: Someone who has the problem you solve AND the money to spend — the first step to getting a customer.
- **Advertising**: "The process of making known" — letting strangers know about the stuff you sell.
- **Grand Slam Offer**: The "so good people feel stupid saying no" offer from $100M Offers; this book assumes you already have one.
- **Acquisition.com**: Hormozi's holding company; portfolio (software, e-commerce, services, brick & mortar, etc.) making $250M+/yr by getting 20,000+ leads/day on offers from $1 to $1M+.
- **Return on advertising (36:1)**: Hormozi's lifetime average — $36 back for every $1 spent (3600%).
- **EBITDA**: The "fancy word for profit" threshold ($1M+/yr) that distinguishes the entrepreneur he invests in vs. the one he helps for free.
- **The book's promise**: "Leads, lots of leads" — solving the "not getting enough leads" problem for good.

## Mental Models
- **Think of advertising as your "get out of jail free card with no expiration date."** It lets you have a terrible product, be terrible at sales, make a ton of mistakes — and still make money. The skill gives you endless chances to get it right, which is priceless in a business world where second chances are rare.
- **Use "double your leads, double your business" as the default lever.** All else being equal, lead volume scales the business linearly — so when growth stalls, ask first whether you're simply not advertising enough.
- **Think of the book's order as zero-to-max skill stacking.** Early sections need no money or skill (get your first client); later sections need both but yield the most leads per unit of time. Masters keep doing the basics — the methods that got you here will likely get you to the next level.
- **Reverse-engineer success.** Hormozi only invests in winners because his models already worked for them — operate on shared, proven trust rather than hope.

## Anti-patterns
- **Skipping to "make money" without offer + leads + sales in place**: Hormozi tried it; it doesn't work. You need all the pieces.
- **Trying to sell your way out of a refund crisis**: In his $150K refund disaster, more selling just created more refunds. The fix was a new lead/offer model, not more brute-force selling.
- **Relying on a single lead source**: Reliability means leads "from lots of places" — one gym owner (or one channel) collapsing shouldn't sink you.
- **Hoarding your advertising secrets**: His biggest breakthrough came from doing the thing that scared him most — giving the system away ($6K licensing sale that became a $25.9M/yr business).
- **Buying the book and never opening it**: Most people do exactly this; finishing puts you in the top 10%.

## Key Takeaways
1. Confirm you have a Grand Slam Offer before chasing leads — this book starts where $100M Offers ends.
2. Treat lead generation as THE survival skill: it's what makes you un-killable and buys you repeated second chances in business.
3. Grade every channel on four axes — More, Better, Cheaper, and Reliably (from many sources).
4. Default growth move: get more customers by getting more leads; doubling leads tends to double the business.
5. Read in order — start with no-money/no-skill tactics to land your first clients, then graduate to the higher-leverage, skill-and-capital-heavy channels.
6. Understand the meta-game: the free book earns trust; the real monetization is Acquisition.com investing equity in $1M+ profit companies.
