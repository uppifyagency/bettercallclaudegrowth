# Chapter 12: Get Lead Getters: Buying Back Your Time with Leverage

## Core Idea
Stop being the only person who does the **Core Four**. Get OTHER people and things — **Lead Getters** — to run the Core Four FOR you, so you get more engaged leads for less of your own work. That is leverage, and it's how you build a $100M Leads machine.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Lead Getters**: The four sources that do the Core Four *on your behalf* — (1) Customers, (2) Employees, (3) Agencies, (4) Affiliates & Partners. They are not part of the Core Four because they're not things you *do*; they're people/businesses you *get*. You still have to run the Core Four to acquire them in the first place.
  - When to use: Once you can reliably get leads yourself and want to scale beyond your own time. Mandatory if you want to reach $100M+.
  - How: Run the Core Four to find a candidate → they become an engaged lead → they get interested in advertising your stuff → they go let *their* people know about you using the Core Four.
- **Work / Leads / Leverage (the Four Scenarios)**: A ladder for evaluating any lead-getting setup by what you put in (Work) versus what you get out (Leads), with Leverage as the ratio.
  - When to use: To diagnose where you are and decide your next move up the ladder.
  - How:
    - Scenario #1 — *You are the Lead Getter.* You run the Core Four yourself all day. Work HIGH, Leads LOW, Leverage LOW.
    - Scenario #2 — *You get a Lead Getter.* One person runs the Core Four for you. Work LOW, Leads LOW, Leverage HIGH.
    - Scenario #3 — *You get lots of Lead Getters.* You spend all day recruiting them; leads climb with each one. Work HIGH, Leads HIGH, Leverage HIGHER.
    - Scenario #4 — *You get a Lead Getter who gets Lead Getters.* You recruit once; they recruit others who advertise for you, and the count grows monthly without you. Work LOW, Leads HIGH, Leverage HIGHEST. → the makings of a $100M Leads machine.

## Key Concepts
- **Leverage**: How much you get for the time you spend getting it. More of what you want, less time spent — that's higher leverage.
- **Lever / fulcrum (Archimedes)**: The mental anchor for leverage — a long enough lever lets an old lady lift a semi-truck the strongest man can't budge barehanded.
- **Lead Getter**: Another person or business that lets prospects know about your stuff using the Core Four, saving you the time of doing it yourself.
- **The Core Four stacks**: You run the Core Four ONCE to get a Lead Getter, then it runs a SECOND time when that Lead Getter gets engaged leads for you. Then it can repeat — Lead Getters get Lead Getters.
- **Every lead can become a Lead Getter**: Lead Getters start as ordinary leads, become engaged leads, then go get *other* people interested too. Ideally every lead converts into one.
- **The four Lead Getters**: Customers (buy, then tell others), Employees (people inside your business who get leads), Agencies (outside businesses with services that get leads), Affiliates (businesses that tell their audiences about you).

## Mental Models
- **Think of a Lead Getter as a second copy of you running the Core Four** — except their time is spent instead of yours, so the same leads cost you less work.
- **Use the Work/Leads/Leverage lens on every growth move**: don't just ask "will this get leads?" — ask "what does it cost me in work, and can someone else carry that work?"
- **Climb toward Scenario #4**: do something *once* so Lead Getters can do it *forever*. One-time effort that produces a compounding, self-recruiting stream beats high-effort grinding.
- **Treat "get leads" and "get Lead Getters" as the same job, not a trade-off** — the answer is "yes, both," because Lead Getters are simply leads who also bring you more leads.

## Anti-patterns
- **Staying in Scenario #1 forever (being your own only Lead Getter)**: Work stays HIGH and Leverage stays LOW. You can pay the bills, but you cap your leads at the limit of your personal hours.
- **Confusing high *activity* with high *leverage***: Scenario #3 (recruiting all day) gets more leads but still buries you in work. More work is not the goal — more leverage is.
- **Treating Lead Getters as part of the Core Four**: You don't "do" affiliates, referrals, agencies, or employees. Trying to "do" them instead of *getting* them keeps the work on your plate.

## Key Takeaways
1. Leverage = output ÷ your time. Choose lead-getting moves that get you more for less of your own hours.
2. There are exactly four Lead Getters — Customers, Employees, Agencies, Affiliates — and all four are higher leverage than you doing the Core Four alone.
3. You must run the Core Four to *acquire* Lead Getters; they don't replace it, they multiply it. The Core Four stacks.
4. Aim for Scenario #4: recruit once, then let Lead Getters recruit more Lead Getters so leads compound monthly without you.
5. Every lead is a potential Lead Getter — design your follow-up so engaged leads start sending you *their* people.
6. Understand all four Lead Getters once and you can build a lead-getting machine for every company you start for life.
