# Chapter 10: Run Paid Ads II: The Money Math (CAC, LTV, CFA)

## Core Idea
Paid ads win on efficiency, not creativity: measure LTGP-to-CAC, aim for at least 3:1, and engineer **Client-Financed Acquisition** so customers pay back your CAC (in cash) within 30 days — making them effectively free and your scale limitless.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Three Phases of Scaling Paid Ads**: the answer to "how much do I spend?"
  - When to use: deciding budget at every maturity stage of an ad account.
  - How:
    1. **Track Money** — set up accurate tracking BEFORE spending a dollar (consultant/tutorials). Without it you "get cleaned out."
    2. **Lose Money** ("investing in a money-printing machine") — you pay first, so the account goes down before it goes up. Expect to lose more times than you win. Budget **2x the 30-day cash collected** per new customer to TEST a new ad (e.g. $100 profit/customer → let it spend up to $200 while getting leads). If an ad gets ZERO leads, kill it before **1x** 30-day cash ($100).
    3. **Print Money** — once ads break even or better, stop asking "how much should I spend?" and ask "how many customers can I handle?" Reverse the budget from your goal.

- **LTGP : CAC Ratio (target 3:1)**: the efficiency benchmark for paid ads.
  - When to use: judging whether ads are profitable and whether to scale.
  - How: LTGP ÷ CAC. Above CAC = profitable; below = losing money. Businesses that struggle to scale almost always sit below 3:1; lifting above 3:1 (lower CAC OR raise LTGP) is when they take off. (A pattern Hormozi observed, not a rule.)

- **Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA)**: the fix for unprofitable cash flow.
  - When to use: when LTGP > CAC over a lifetime, but the first purchase doesn't cover CAC, starving your cash for more ads.
  - How: make the customer pay back the cost to **get + fulfill** them within **30 days** (chosen because any business can get 30 days of interest-free money via a credit card). Square the balance, owe zero, keep a customer who profits forever, then repeat. `$30 CAC - $30 cash collected within 30 days = free customers.`

- **Reverse-Budget From Sales Goals**: in Phase Three, spend = (customers you can handle) x CAC, then pad ~20% because ads get less efficient as they scale.
  - How: handle 100 customers at $100 CAC = $10,000; +20% = $12,000/30 days = $400/day. "If the number terrifies you, you're doing it right."

## Key Concepts

- **CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer)**: total spend divided by customers acquired — and it doesn't only come from advertising.
- **LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)**: all money a customer ever spends minus all costs to deliver — Hormozi's preferred metric over "LTV"/"Lifetime Value."
- **Gross Profit**: revenue minus cost of delivery (e.g. $15 sale - $5 cost = $10) — the actual cash used to acquire customers, pay rent, and run everything.
- **30-Day Cash Collected**: gross profit collected from a customer in their first 30 days — the basis for both test budgets and CFA (NOT full LTGP).
- **Break-Even Window**: the first 30 days, in which CFA aims to recoup CAC.
- **Upsell as CFA Lever**: an extra offer that pulls future LTGP forward into the 30-day window (e.g. $100 upsell at 100% margin taken by 1 in 5 = +$20/customer).
- **Industry-Average CAC**: the diagnostic baseline. Costs between competitors in the same industry are closer than people think; winners differ by how much they make per customer.

## Mental Models

- **Think of paid ads like a casino where you become the house.** You lose often (small) while learning; you win rarely (big) by spotting winners and 100x-ing down on them. Buy a dollar, sell it for two.
- **Use industry-average CAC to diagnose ads vs. business model.** If your CAC is below 3x the average, your ads are fine — fix LTGP (business model). If CAC is above 3x average, fix your advertising.
- **Costs approach zero; revenue approaches infinity.** Saving $100 off CAC eventually costs more effort than earning an extra $100 per customer. Past a point, stop optimizing CAC and grow LTGP — you can't "save your way" to a billion.
- **Think of profitability over a long horizon.** It can take a year and real "wasted" money to make ads profitable; the winner then earns it all back in the next month. It's normal — a money machine shouldn't be cheap or easy.

## Anti-patterns

- **Spending before tracking is set up**: like gambling until you feel like stopping instead of until you can afford to — you get cleaned out.
- **Killing winners too early**: stopping at a visible loss (e.g. -$500 across 10 tests) blinds you to the one ad worth scaling 100x.
- **Letting losers run too long**: emotionally bleeding budget into ads that already "suck" instead of cutting at the 1x/2x 30-day-cash thresholds.
- **Confusing a sales problem with an advertising problem**: if engaged leads have the problem and the money but don't buy, your ads work — your sales are broken. One owner mislabeled this and lost ~$30M in enterprise value, "six inches from gold."
- **Saying "I'm not techy"**: a self-fulfilling tax that keeps you poorer; Hormozi wasted four years before fixing his site in four hours.
- **Treating creativity/copy as the lever**: tweaking "perfect" copy while ignoring the numbers — efficiency, not artistry, is what scales.

| Step | Math | 30-Day Cash |
|---|---|---|
| First-month gross profit | $15 − $5 | $10 |
| Add upsell (1 in 5 take $100, 100% margin) | $100 ÷ 5 | +$20 |

| Net within 30 days | $30 − $30 | **$0 → free customer** |

Budget thresholds & scaling

| Situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| Testing a new ad (getting leads) | spend up to 2x 30-day cash, then stop |
| New ad getting zero leads | kill before 1x 30-day cash |
| CAC below 3x industry average | ads are fine → fix LTGP |
| CAC above 3x industry average | fix advertising (CAC) |
| Phase Three scaling | (customers you can handle × CAC) + 20% |

## Key Takeaways

1. Set up tracking before spending a single dollar; you can't scale what you can't measure.
2. Expect to lose more often than you win — keep losses small (cut at 1x–2x 30-day cash) and pour money into the rare big winners.
3. Measure LTGP : CAC, not raw ad cost; drive it above 3:1 by lowering CAC, raising LTGP, or both.
4. Use industry-average CAC to know what to fix: below 3x average → it's your business model; above 3x → it's your ads.
5. Engineer Client-Financed Acquisition: recoup the cost to get AND fulfill a customer within 30 days (often via an upsell) so customers are effectively free and cash recycles into the next customer.
6. Once ads break even, reverse the budget from how many customers you can handle, pad ~20%, and commit.
7. Don't blame ads for a sales problem — and place a real $100 ad now to get in the game.
