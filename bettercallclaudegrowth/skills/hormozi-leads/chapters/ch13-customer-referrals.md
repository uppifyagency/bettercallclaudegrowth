# Chapter 13: Customer Referrals: Word of Mouth

## Core Idea
Referrals are the lowest-cost, highest-profit, best-quality lead source and the only one that scales exponentially — but you only earn them by building so much goodwill that customers want to tell friends, and then explicitly asking. "The best advertising is a happy customer."

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Referral Growth Equation**: Referrals (in) minus churned customers (out).
  - When to use: To diagnose whether word of mouth can grow your business on its own.
  - How: Compare monthly referral % to churn %. Referrals > churn = you grow with no other advertising (compounding). Referrals = churn = you need other advertising to grow. Referrals < churn = you advertise just to break even (the "hamster wheel of death" — where most businesses live).

- **Six Ways To Get More Referrals By Giving More Value** (maps to the parts of an ad):
  - When to use: To raise goodwill (value minus price) so the product becomes remarkable enough to refer.
  - How: 1) **Call Outs → Sell Better Customers**: find what your lowest-churn / best-result customers have in common, then target only that "perfect fit" audience. 2) **Dream Outcome → Set Better Expectations**: slowly lower the promises in your offer until close rates dip, then stop — leaves room to overdeliver. 3) **Increase Perceived Likelihood of Achievement → Get More People Better Results**: survey/interview best customers, find common actions, force new customers to repeat them, match guarantee conditions to those actions. 4) **Decrease Time Delay → Make Faster Wins**: break outcomes into smaller increments, give wins more often. 5) **Decrease Effort & Sacrifice → Keep Making Your Stuff Better**: run a monthly loop fixing the most common product problem. 6) **Call to Action → Tell Them What To Buy Next**: keep selling front AND back end so they have more to refer to.

- **One Question to Rule Them All**: "You've lost all customers but one, the advertising gods ban the Core Four, and ALL new customers must come from this one customer — how would you treat them?"
  - When to use: As a team thought experiment to force product/experience excellence.
  - How: Define the results, onboarding, and customer type that would make this one person refer everyone they know. Then do it, acting as if Core Four privileges could be revoked at any moment.

- **Seven Ways To Ask For Referrals** (a referral program = how you give the incentive + what you incentivize with + how you ask):
  - When to use: After goodwill exists, to capitalize on it and trigger viral growth.
  - How: 1) **One-Sided Benefit** (pay your CAC to referrer OR friend). 2) **Two-Sided Benefit** (split CAC: half to referrer in cash/credit, half to friend in credit — Dropbox/PayPal model). 3) **Ask Right When They Buy** (on contract/checkout, get names + numbers, frame "do it with a friend = better results"). 4) **Negotiation Chip** (trade a discount for a 3-way introduction — ethically different price for different terms). 5) **Referral Events** (1–4 week time-boxed promos: "Bring-a-Friend", "Spouse Challenge", "Accountability Buddy"). 6) **Ongoing Referral Programs** (talk up "do it with others" everywhere, always). 7) **Unlockable Referral Bonuses** (non-cash: VIP access, courses, status, merch, premium support — for those who refer AND leave a testimonial).

## Key Concepts
- **Goodwill**: The gap between value (what they get) and price (what they charge); economists call it "customer surplus." More goodwill → word of mouth → referrals.
- **Referral**: When a referrer sends an engaged lead to your business; the best referrals come from customers.
- **Exponential vs. linear growth**: Core Four leads are linear (double inputs ≈ double leads); word of mouth is exponential (1 brings 2, 2 bring 4) — nothing scales like it.
- **A "win"**: Any positive experience a customer has; faster/more frequent wins raise perceived speed, retention, and trust (a "triple win").
- **BAMFAM**: Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting — never leave a customer in "no man's land"; they should always know when they'll hear from you next.
- **First 48 hours**: Customers form their lasting impression within two days of buying — front-load wins into that window.
- **Asking-as-an-offer**: Referral asks only work when framed as an offer showing the value the customer gets for referring.

## Mental Models
- **Think of referrals as both higher LTGP and lower CAC**: referred customers buy more, more often, pay upfront, AND cost nothing. A 4:1 LTGP:CAC where each customer brings two more becomes effectively 12:1 — three customers for the price of one.
- **Use the embarrassment test when referrals are missing**: ask "Why are my customers too embarrassed to tell everyone about my product?" If direct-to-consumer customers aren't bringing more customers, your product has room to improve — it's unremarkable, "not worthy of remark."
- **Think of price as a temporary lever and value as the real one**: anyone can lower price ("Any fool can sell something for less" — Rory Sutherland). The durable question is "how do we give MORE value?"
- **Use "deliver early, never late"**: add 50% to timelines so your "on time" is their "early." Never expect a customer to forgive you.

## Anti-patterns
- **"Everyone loves our stuff, we just need to get the word out"**: If the product were exceptional you'd already have more business than you could handle. Lack of referrals is a product problem, not an awareness problem.
- **Never asking for referrals**: The single biggest reason businesses get far fewer referrals than they could — customers, like any audience, only act when told to.
- **Obsessing over the front end and neglecting the back end**: Customers who fall off your product don't refer; keep selling them what to buy next.
- **Over-promising to close**: Inflated promises become a fulfillment nightmare and destroy goodwill; quietly lower promises while holding quality.
- **Treating referrals as a one-off hack**: Referrals are a way of doing business, not a trick you "do."

## Key Takeaways
1. Fix the product before chasing reach — build goodwill (value > price) until customers are compelled to tell others.
2. Compute your referral % and churn % to set a baseline; getting referrals above churn is what unlocks self-sustaining, compounding growth.
3. Sell only your "perfect fit" customers (lowest churn / best results) — they extract the most value and refer like clockwork.
4. Find what your best-result customers did differently, then force new customers to repeat it and tie your guarantee to those actions.
5. Give wins faster and more often; communicate constantly (updates are wins) and always book the next touchpoint (BAMFAM).
6. Frame the referral ask as an offer with a clear incentive, ask right at the point of purchase, and make a true 3-way introduction (not just a name and number).
7. Pay up to your CAC as the referral incentive; non-cash unlockable bonuses cost less and can reward both parties.
