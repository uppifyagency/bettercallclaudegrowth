# Chapter 5: Warm Outreach: Reaching People You Know

## Core Idea
Warm outreach is 1-to-1 contact with people who already know you (the first of the Core Four), and it's the cheapest, most reliable way to get your First Five Clients. The whole machine: build a list, reach out to ~100 people every day, warm them with A-C-A, make a free offer, then start charging once referrals come.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Warm Outreach (the 10 Steps)**: the full sequence for working a warm audience.
  - When to use: getting your "First Five Clients" for any new product/service; advanced operators use it for re-engagement and new product lines.
  - How: (1) Get your list (2) Pick a platform (3) Personalize your message (4) Reach out to 100/day (5) Warm them up (6) Invite their friends (7) Make the easiest offer in the world (free) (8) Start back at the top (9) Start charging (10) Keep your list warm.

- **A-C-A Framework**: how to reply when someone responds, so you break the ice without sounding icky.
  - When to use: every reply in a warm conversation, to guide it toward your offer.
  - How: **A**cknowledge what they said (restate in your own words = active listening) -> **C**ompliment them, tied to a positive character trait -> **A**sk another question that steers toward a topic near your offer. People love talking about themselves and being complimented; do both and they'll like and trust you more.

- **The Value Equation (recall from $100M Offers)**: the lens for building an offer from scratch.
  - When to use: Step 6, when you decide to make the offer.
  - How: maximize the top two — **Dream Outcome** (what they want, how they want it) and **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** (proof: results, reviews, certs, guarantees) — and minimize the bottom two — **Time Delay** (how fast results come) and **Effort and Sacrifice** (the bad stuff to endure / good stuff to give up). Show them: you have exactly what they want, guaranteed, insanely fast, without lifting a finger.

- **The "Do you know anybody?" offer script**: ask if they know anyone who's struggling and wants the dream outcome, mention you're taking on 5 free case studies for testimonials, name-drop a result from someone like them, end with "Does anyone you like come to mind?" (and if no: "does anyone you hate come to mind? ha" to break awkwardness).
  - Why it works: you're not asking them to buy — only if they know someone. Most who say yes turn out to be interested themselves. Wins three ways: they show interest, they refer, or both.

- **The 9-Word Email (Dean Jackson)**: a re-engagement probe for Step 10. Format: "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?" No images, no links, no frills — just the question. Replies = engaged leads, and they become your top priority for warm reach outs.

## Key Concepts
- **Warm reach out**: 1-to-1 contact (call, text, email, DM, voicemail, mail) with people who already know you.
- **Warm audience**: everyone who knows you — your phone contacts, every email address book, all social followers/connections. Everybody knows somebody.
- **Your list**: the sum of contacts across all platforms; for many beginners this is their first 1,000 leads.
- **Hidden costs**: the time, effort, and sacrifice to get results (the bottom of the Value Equation). If people won't take it even for free, your "free" stuff is too expensive.
- **The "no tax"**: failures you must pay down to reach yeses — "Yeses give me opportunity. Nos give me feedback. Either way, I win."
- **First Five Free**: give the first five customers of any new product/service the offer for free to get reps, forgive your early suckiness, learn fast, and generate reviews/referrals.
- **Free customer math**: free customers still make money 3 ways — they convert to paying, they refer paying customers, and their testimonials attract paying customers.
- **Engaged lead**: a contact who responds/shows interest (e.g., a reply to the 9-word email) — see Ch2.

## Mental Models
- **Think of your contacts as already-permissioned leads**: every phone contact subscribed to communication from you — they gave you the means and permission to reach out. "I have no leads" is almost never true.
- **Use FREE as the easiest offer enhancer when you're new**: don't try to look advanced — people aren't dumb. Free calms your nerves, buys forgiveness while you "suck less," and manufactures social proof.
- **Treat "no" as information, not rejection**: ask "Why?" and "What would I have to do to make it worth it to continue?" Their answer either wins them or gives you ammo for the next person.
- **Think of a warm list as an asset that feeds you forever**: keep giving value so it stays primed; a warm list is a consistent, growing source of engaged leads.

## Anti-patterns
- **Skipping warm outreach because it doesn't scale instantly**: most businesses don't do it — that's exactly why it works. It's reliable "as certain as the sun rises."
- **Being a weirdo / pushy in the personalization step**: pay your social dues, lead with genuine value, ask for nothing first.
- **Asking people to buy too early**: the offer asks "do you know anyone?", not "will you buy?" — that's what keeps you from coming off pushy.
- **Working for free forever**: free is a starting tactic, not a model. Once referrals start, that's your litmus test that you're good enough to charge.
- **Not reaching the daily volume**: under ~100/day across 3 attempts, the benchmark math collapses; volume is what guarantees a customer.

## Reference Tables

Warm outreach benchmarks (per 100 reach outs):

| Stage | Conversion | Result from 100 |
|---|---|---|
| Reach outs -> replies/engaged | ~1 in 5 | ~20 reply |
| Replies -> take free offer | ~1 in 5 | ~4 free |
| Free -> paid (later) | ~1 in 4 | ~1 customer |

Income math (warm outreach only): 500 reach outs/week -> ~5 customers/week. 5 customers x $400 product = $2,000/wk. $2,000 x 52 = $104,000/yr (assumes ~1% of list buys a $400 offer).

Pricing ramp after free: free -> 80% off next five -> 60% off -> 40% off -> keep raising 20% every five until your sweet spot (the rising price also adds urgency).

## Key Takeaways
1. Build your list first: pull every contact from your phone, all email accounts, and all social platforms — that's your first ~1,000 leads.
2. Pick the platform with the most contacts, personalize each message, and reach out to 100 people per day, up to 3 times each (3 days, or once/week for physical mail).
3. When they reply, run A-C-A — Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask — to build trust and steer toward your offer over ~3-4 exchanges.
4. Make the offer as "do you know anybody?" + free for the first five, set expectations (use it, give feedback, leave a review).
5. When someone says no, ask why and what it'd take — convert rejection into either a sale or feedback.
6. Once people start referring, start charging, raising price every five sales.
7. Keep the list warm with regular value and periodic 9-word emails; the limits are your time (4+ hrs/day starting out) and the finite number of people who know you — when you run out, move to posting free content.
