# Chapter 4: The Core Four: The Only Four Ways to Get Leads

## Core Idea
There are only four ways one person can let other people know about anything, and therefore only four ways to get leads. Combine two audience types (warm vs. cold) with two communication modes (1-to-1 vs. 1-to-many) and you get the Core Four. Everything else in advertising is just a variation, automation, or scaling of these four actions.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Core Four**: The 2x2 of {Warm, Cold} audience x {1-to-1, 1-to-many} communication. The four cells are the ONLY methods that exist for generating engaged leads:
  - 1-to-1 to a Warm audience = **Warm Outreach**
  - 1-to-many to a Warm audience = **Post Free Content** (Posting Content)
  - 1-to-1 to a Cold audience = **Cold Outreach**
  - 1-to-many to a Cold audience = **Run Paid Ads**
  - When to use: Always. This is the master map for every lead-generation decision in the book. If you aren't getting enough leads, you're not doing the Core Four with enough skill or enough volume.
  - How: (1) Pick which audience you can reach (warm = permission; cold = strangers). (2) Pick the communication mode you can sustain. (3) Execute that cell's method. (4) Add more methods over time rather than abandoning one.

## Key Concepts
- **Warm audience**: People who gave you permission to contact them — friends, family, followers, current customers, past customers, contacts. "People who know you."
- **Cold audience**: People who have NOT given you permission to contact them — strangers reached via bought/built contact lists or paid platform access. "Other people's audiences."
- **1-to-1 (Private)**: Only one person gets a message at a time. Think phone call or email.
- **1-to-many (Public)**: Many people receive the message simultaneously. Think social posts, billboards, podcasts.
- **Automation**: Machines doing some of the work. It does NOT change the nature of a communication — emailing 10,000 people is still 1-to-1, just done "really fast by a machine."
- **Engaged lead**: The output of any Core Four method — a lead who has shown interest (carried over from Ch2).

## Mental Models
- **Think of the Core Four as a "get out of jail free" card.** Once internalized, it gives you unlimited chances to succeed at business — there is always one of four levers you can pull or pull harder.
- **Use the warm/cold split to decide your opening move.** Permission changes everything about how you advertise — warm audiences tolerate direct contact; cold audiences require you to earn or buy attention first.
- **Don't let automation fool you into inventing a "fifth way."** Classify any tactic by its true nature: who's the audience (warm/cold) and how many receive it at once (1-to-1 / 1-to-many). It will always land in one of the four cells.
- **Treat "not enough leads" as a skill-or-volume diagnosis, never a strategy problem.** The four actions are fixed; only your execution quality and quantity vary.

## Anti-patterns
- **Believing advertising is complicated**: The industry overcomplicates a system that reduces to four core actions. Confusion is a tax you pay for not knowing the map.
- **Letting automation blur your categories**: Calling automated email "one-to-many" because it reaches thousands — it's still private, 1-to-1 communication and must be optimized as such.
- **Hopping between methods without building skill**: Each method takes you one step closer to overflowing leads only if you do it with enough skill and volume; abandoning a cell early forfeits the payoff.

## Reference Tables

The Core Four (the only four ways to get leads):

| | **1-to-1 (Private)** | **1-to-many (Public)** |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm audience** (people who know you / gave permission) | Warm Outreach | Post Free Content |
| **Cold audience** (strangers / no permission) | Cold Outreach | Run Paid Ads |

## Key Takeaways
1. Memorize the Core Four and make it part of yourself — it is referenced throughout the rest of the book.
2. First ask "warm or cold?" (permission?), then "1-to-1 or 1-to-many?" (private or public?). Those two questions name your method.
3. Automation and scale are amplifiers of the four methods, not new methods — classify by underlying nature.
4. If leads are short, the fix is more skill or more volume in the Core Four, not a new tactic.
5. Either do the Core Four well, or get crushed by those who do — there is no neutral option.
