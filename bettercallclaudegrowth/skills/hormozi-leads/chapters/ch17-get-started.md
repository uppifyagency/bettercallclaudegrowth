# Chapter 17: Get Started: Open to Goal, The Roadmap & The $100M Lead Machine

## Core Idea
Advertising is an inputs-and-outputs game won by volume and persistence: commit to outcomes ("Open to Goal"), follow the seven-level roadmap that adds methods and Lead Getters in their natural order, and never stop rolling the die — "You cannot lose if you do not quit."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Open to Goal**: Don't commit to *doing* an action N times (that's the Rule of 100) — commit to *the work until you hit a specific number of outcomes*, no matter what. Work until the GOAL is hit, not until the clock says stop. "If some is good, more is better."
  - When to use: After the Rule of 100 has become your norm and you're ready for the next level of effort to find or scale a winner.
  - How: Set a daily outcome target (e.g., 5 new members/day). Keep working that day until you hit it — whether it takes until lunch or 18 hours. Give up "doing your best"; do what is required.

- **One Page Advertising Checklist**: The simplest daily action plan, fillable in ~5 minutes, that leaves no room for excuses.
  - When to use: Daily, to structure your focused work block on a single page instead of a 100-page plan that never gets used.
  - How (5 steps): 1) Pick the engaged-lead type to get (Customers, Affiliates, Employees, or Agencies). 2) Pick Rule of 100 or Open to Goal and commit to daily advertising actions. 3) Fill out the advertising checklist for that daily action. 4) Do it daily until you earn enough to pay someone else to do it. 5) Then go back to Step 1, make Employees your new target lead type, repeat steps 1-4, and scale again.

- **The Roadmap (Seven Levels of Advertisers)**: The order of operations to go from zero to a $100M+ lead machine. See Reference Table.
  - When to use: To identify where you are on the "advertising totem pole" and what to do to reach the next level.
  - How: Start with one offer, one avatar, one platform; add Content + Warm Outreach; then add Employees, Referrals, more methods/platforms, and finally veteran executives.

## Key Concepts
- **Open to Goal vs. Rule of 100**: Rule of 100 commits to a fixed *number of inputs*; Open to Goal commits to a fixed *number of outcomes* — "Rule of 100 on Steroids."
- **Volume**: The single missing ingredient most people lack — they do the right action in the wrong (too-small) amount and quit. 300 flyers is 1/1500th the required effort; 100 reach-outs over six weeks is 1/42 of "100 per day."
- **Inputs and outputs game**: You input advertising effort; your output is engaged leads. Outputs are not out of your control — low inputs guarantee low, unreliable outputs.
- **Habit stack (Open to Goal in practice)**: Wake early (4-5am), get right to work (no rituals), no meetings until noon — a long stretch of uninterrupted work after a long stretch of uninterrupted sleep.
- **Test budget for education**: Set aside a fixed % (1%, 5%, 10%) of ad budget to test new campaigns, channels, pages, or crazy ideas without expecting a return — "permission to fail." You either win or you learn.
- **The $100M+ Lead Machine**: The full system view of a $100M+ business where advertising fires on all cylinders across every Core Four method and all four Lead Getters, run by an executive team without you.
- **The Many-Sided Die**: A fable — more rolls (more methods, more attempts) = more chances to hit green; every die hits its green streak when rolled enough. More methods = more chances to win.
- **Double down until it breaks**: Test until something works, take massive action, stay focused, double down until it breaks, then test for the next thing and double down on that.

## Mental Models
- **Think of advertising as the Many-Sided Die**: You can't see how many sides you have or whether you're on roll #100 or #100,000; you only see red or green. Each green turns one red side green, so wins compound. You'll hit red on your first rolls — keep rolling, because the only two guarantees are "the more you roll, the better you get" and "if you quit, you lose."
- **Use Open to Goal when scaling beyond personal capacity**: When effort is the variable you can fully control, refuse to let effort be the reason anything fails. Blame the offer, copy, image, targeting, media, or platform — never your effort.
- **Think in decade-long horizons**: People try shortcuts for a decade until they realize they should have picked one strategy and stuck with it for a decade. Expect 3-6 months to crack a new lead source; 5-10 years to a $100M machine. "Play games where if you wait, you win."
- **Use the Roadmap to diagnose your constraint**: Each level has one Primary Action. Don't skip ahead — the biggest mistake is letting your product slip at Level 4 (referrals) while chasing more advertising.

## Anti-patterns
- **Stopping too soon / underestimating volume**: Doing 1/1500th or 1/42 of the required effort, then concluding "it doesn't work." The right action in the wrong amount still fails.
- **Get-rich-quick obsession**: Expecting to be a millionaire in year one ensures it never happens; quitting or switching methods after a few losses kills inevitable success.
- **Letting the product slip at Level 4**: Most people chase more ads while their product (and referral engine) decays, and never recover.
- **The 100-page plan**: Writing pages of unused "baloney" instead of a single-page checklist that exposes whether you did the work or not.
- **Maxing personal capacity but never adding people**: Refusing to hire (Employees) or manage people outside the business (Affiliates) caps growth permanently.

## Key Takeaways
1. Switch from Rule of 100 (fixed inputs) to Open to Goal (fixed outcomes) once volume is your norm — work until the goal is hit, not until you're tired.
2. Make effort the one variable you never blame: do the right action in massive enough volume before judging whether it works.
3. Fill out the One Page Advertising Checklist daily (pick lead type → pick Rule of 100/Open to Goal → fill the checklist → do it until you can hire → repeat with Employees).
4. Follow the Roadmap in order: Warm Outreach → add Content → add Employees → secure Referrals (protect the product) → multiple methods/platforms → veteran executives.
5. Set aside a fixed % of ad budget purely to test new things without expecting a return — "you either win or you learn."
6. Build the $100M Machine on a 5-10 year horizon; expect 3-6 months to crack any new lead source.
7. Play the Many-Sided Die: keep rolling through the red, double down on every green until it breaks, and never quit.
