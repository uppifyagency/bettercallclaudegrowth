# Chapter 16: Affiliates & Partners: Building an Affiliate Army

## Core Idea
An affiliate is a Lead Getter who runs their own business and advertises your stuff to their engaged leads in exchange for money or free stuff. Recruit an Affiliate Army and each one becomes a self-multiplying stream of leads, so in theory you may never need to advertise directly again.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **The 6-Step Affiliate Playbook**: the full sequence to build an Affiliate Army.
  - When to use: Lead Getter #4 — when you have a working offer and want compounding, leveraged lead flow.
  - How: (1) Find your ideal affiliates → (2) Make them an offer → (3) Qualify them → (4) Figure out what to pay them → (5) Get them advertising (Launch) → (6) Keep them advertising (Integrate).

- **"Who's got my leads?!"** (Step 1 — Find ideal affiliates): list businesses whose warm audience is full of people like your customers.
  - How: Ask of your best customers — What do they buy? → who provides it. Where do they go? → what businesses surround that. What do they like to do? → who provides it. For D2C, target the employers of your consumers. Sort hits into categories: softwares, products, equipment, services, groups they belong to, events they attend. A business in multiple categories is likely a great affiliate.

- **The Affiliate Offer** (Step 2): don't offer your product — offer a fast, simple, easy way to make commissions promoting it.
  - How: Call out the affiliate, their customers, the results/products they deliver, or your own customers. Then show value elements: more money from current customers + more leads (dream outcome), high likelihood (their customers already want it), without building/delivering/supporting it (effort & sacrifice), starting tomorrow (time delay).

- **Three-Tier Payout Structure** (Step 4): scale payout to commitment so blended cost stays below max allowable CAC.
  - How: Set max allowable CAC from your LTGP:CAC target. Tier 1 = 25% CAC (signs up, buys product/cert); Tier 2 = 50% CAC (activates — finishes cert, does a launch); Tier 3 = 100% CAC (sustains performance, e.g. 5 subscription customers/month). Top affiliates earn full CAC; the blended average stays lower, leaving leftover profit for contests, recruiting, or pocketing.

- **Whisper-Tease-Shout** (Step 5 — Launch): a 3-phase launch to activate affiliates (works for launching anything).
  - How: **Whisper** = call-outs, pure curiosity, show behind-the-scenes; start years/60 days out (every 4-6 wks, then every 2-3 wks at 30 days). **Tease** = reveal product, set date, show elements of value with What-Who-When (once/wk to 14 days, twice/wk to 3 days). **Shout** = call to action with bonuses, scarcity, urgency, "be first" (twice/day from 3 days out, every few hours on launch day, every 30 min near go-live).

- **The Integration Upsell Chain** (Step 6 — Keep them advertising): three ways to bolt your product into their offer, easiest to hardest.
  - How: (1) Affiliate **gives away your Lead Magnet** with every purchase of their stuff → you upsell your core offer. (2) Affiliate **sells your Lead Magnet** (they keep the cash) → you upsell your core offer. (3) Affiliate **sells your core offer directly** → you split the money forever, never cap payouts.

## Key Concepts
- **Affiliate**: an independent business / Lead Getter that advertises your stuff to its own engaged leads for money, free stuff, or both.
- **Affiliate Army**: many affiliates recruited, activated, and integrated — each adding a compounding stream of leads.
- **Super-affiliate**: an affiliate who brings you other affiliates (a third layer), e.g. ALAN's agency super-affiliates → agencies → local businesses.
- **Make Them a Customer**: lowest-barrier qualifier — affiliates must buy (and ideally use) the product to keep status; the more they invest, the more they make.
- **Make Them an Expert**: charge for onboarding/certification at 10-20% of what an average active affiliate makes in their first 12 months ($4k-$8k if they'll earn ~$40k/yr); covers ad costs and funds proper training.
- **Launch then Integrate**: launch creates a one-time activation spike; integration converts it into enduring lead flow.
- **Affiliate LTGP:CAC**: compare cost to get an affiliate against the gross profit of all customers they send (not profit from the affiliate themselves).

## Mental Models
- **Think of affiliates as a second layer of customers** — the people who get you customers. Treat them like customers: make the offer so good they'd feel stupid saying no, and get them their first win fast.
- **Use "if they pay, they'll pay attention"** — get affiliates invested (time, money, or in the product) so they're forced to win quickly; if they won't buy your stuff, they shouldn't sell it.
- **Use the leverage math** when you doubt affiliates are worth it: 10 customers/month plateaus a business at $1.2M/yr; 10 *affiliates*/month (same effort) compounds to $7.8M and keeps growing.
- **Be a winner**: "I have to make affiliates work for my business," not "affiliates can't work for my business." Advertising always works — it's only a matter of efficiency.

## Anti-patterns
- **Giving away the whole farm (full CAC) to every affiliate**: not all affiliates are equal; flat max payouts destroy the leftover profit a tiered structure preserves.
- **Setting onboarding/cert price too low or too high**: too low and affiliates aren't invested; too high and you won't get enough — 10-20% of first-year earnings maximizes active affiliates.
- **Recruiting affiliates and leaving them to figure out the launch**: good launches have the work done ahead of time — hand them a plug-and-play whisper-tease-shout kit.
- **Stopping at the launch**: without integration, the spike fades; integration is the long-term strategy for enduring lead flow.

## Reference Tables

### Three-Tier Payout (example: $40 max allowable CAC)
| Tier | Trigger | % of CAC | Payout |
|------|---------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Agrees to terms (signs up, buys product/cert) | 25% | $10 |
| 2 | Activates (finishes cert, does posts/outreach, runs a launch) | 50% | $20 |
| 3 | Sustains (e.g. 5 subscription customers/month) | 100% | $40 |

If mix = 20% / 20% / 60%, blended payout = $30 (not $40), lifting LTGP:CAC from 3:1 to 4:1.

### Affiliate Returns Math (widget company example)
| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| CAC to get one affiliate | $4,000 |
| Affiliate sells | $10,000/mo × 12 mo = $120,000 |
| Cost of goods (25%) | $30,000 |
| Gross profit from their customers | $90,000 |
| Affiliate payout (40% of GP) | $36,000 |
| Leftover gross profit | $54,000 |
| **Affiliate LTGP:CAC** | **$54,000 / $4,000 = 12.5 : 1** |

Fix a sub-3:1 ratio by: (1) Lower CAC (better ads/offer/sales), (2) Increase LTGP & decrease CAC (better launch process), (3) Increase LTGP (better integration).

## Key Takeaways
1. Don't sell affiliates your product — sell them a fast, easy way to earn commissions promoting it; make them the "customer" you advertise to.
2. Qualify by getting affiliates invested: make them a customer, make them an expert (paid certification), or both.
3. Pay only for outcomes you want (new + repeat customers) and use a three-tier payout so your blended cost stays well under max allowable CAC.
4. Activate every affiliate with a Whisper-Tease-Shout launch, doing all the prep work so they can plug and play.
5. Convert one-time launches into forever lead flow via the Integration upsell chain (give away → sell → sell core offer), splitting money forever and never capping payouts.
6. Score affiliates by comparing CAC-to-recruit against the gross profit of all customers they send — aim for 5:1, 10:1+.
7. Start small: advertise your affiliate offer until you get 10-20 affiliates, fix the kinks, then turn their results into your first batch of affiliate Lead Magnets and scale.
