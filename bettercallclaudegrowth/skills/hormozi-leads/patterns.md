# Patterns — $100M Leads (Alex Hormozi)

Repeatable techniques and step-by-step playbooks. Preserve exact names.

## Seven Steps To Creating an Effective Lead Magnet
**When to use**: When you struggle to get leads, or when you sell more expensive stuff and people want to know more before they buy. Advertise the core offer first; if engagement is low, lead with a lead magnet.
**How**:
1. Figure out the problem you want to solve and who to solve it for (use the Problem-Solution cycle: pick a narrow, meaningful problem whose *next* problem your core offer solves).
2. Figure out how to solve it (pick one of the 3 Lead Magnet types).
3. Figure out how to deliver it (software, information, services, physical product).
4. Test what to name it (test headline first, then image, then subheadline; run polls).
5. Make it easy to consume (package in every format — image/video/text/audio).
6. Make it darn good — give away the secrets, sell the implementation (provide more value than the cost of your core offer, before they buy).
7. Make it easy for them to tell you they want more (a CTA = what to do + a reason to do it now).
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Make as many versions per delivery method as possible (up to 12) and rotate them. A $25 lead-magnet budget can 10x engaged leads vs. $0 and cut CAC ~3x (example: $3000 CAC → $1000 CAC = 10:1 return, triples the business).

## The 3 Lead Magnet Types
**When to use**: Step 2 of the lead-magnet system — choosing how to solve the narrow problem.
**How**:
1. **Reveal Their Problem** ("diagnosis") — best when problems worsen the longer you wait (speed test, posture analysis, termite inspection).
2. **Samples And Trials** — full but brief access to the core offer; best when the core offer is a recurring solution to a recurring problem (free adjustment, food samples, limited-time/limited-use trial).
3. **One Step Of A Multi-Step Process** — give one valuable step free, rest on purchase; best for complex problems (free first coat of sealant; free finance calculators).
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: All three solve one problem and reveal others. Lead magnet must be valuable enough you *could* charge for it, and leave them wanting more.

## The A-C-A Reply Framework
**When to use**: When a warm (or cold, via DM) contact responds; to break the ice and steer toward your offer without being icky.
**How**:
1. **Acknowledge** what they said — restate in your own words (shows active listening).
2. **Compliment** them — tie it to a positive character trait.
3. **Ask** another question — lead the conversation toward your offer.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: People love talking about themselves and being complimented; this builds like/trust. Get through ~3-4 exchanges (or 3-4 min in person) before making an offer.

## Warm Outreach (10 Steps)
**When to use**: To get your first five clients for any new product/service; cheapest and most reliable channel; also re-engagement and new product lines.
**How**:
1. Get your list (phone, email, all social platforms — everyone has 1000+ contacts).
2. Pick a platform (the one with the most contacts).
3. Personalize your greeting (use something you know about them; no ask yet).
4. Reach out to 100 people per day; up to 3 times (once/day for 3 days, or until reply; physical mail weekly).
5. Warm them up using the A-C-A framework.
6. Make them an offer using the value equation (Dream Outcome, Perceived Likelihood, Time Delay, Effort & Sacrifice) — phrase it as "do you know anybody who…" so you're not asking them to buy.
7. Make it easy to say yes — make it free (first five free, in exchange for: use it, give feedback, leave a review).
8. Start at the top — after one platform, move to the next-most-contacts platform.
9. Start charging — once people start referring, swap "free" for "80% off for the next five," then 60%, 40%… raise 20% every five.
10. Keep your list warm — give regular value; probe with Dean Jackson's 9-word email: "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?"
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: ~1 in 5 contacts engage (100 → ~20 replies → ~4 take the free offer → ~1 paid customer). 500 reach outs/week × $400 product at 1% buy ≈ $104,000/yr. Limiters: your time (4+ hrs/day) and audience size.

## The Content Unit (Hook - Retain - Reward)
**When to use**: Whenever creating any audience-growing free content; the smallest unit that can grow an audience (string units together for longer content).
**How**:
1. **Hook** attention — give them a reason to notice. Use Topics (Far Past, Recent Past, Present, Trending, Manufactured), Headlines (include 2+ of: Recency, Relevancy, Celebrity, Proximity, Conflict, Unusual, Ongoing), and Format (match the platform's best content).
2. **Retain** attention — drive curiosity by embedding questions via Lists, Steps, and Stories.
3. **Reward** attention — satisfy the reason they consumed it; think value per second; completely answer the embedded questions.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: "There's no such thing as too long, only too boring." Success metric: your audience grows (measure absolute and % growth monthly). Switch "How to" → "How I." Avoid pre-scheduling; manually post.

## Give-Give-Give, Until They Ask
**When to use**: Monetizing a warm audience via posted content without killing growth.
**How**:
1. Over-give with rewarding content; deposit goodwill before withdrawing with asks.
2. Maintain a high give:ask ratio (3:1 minimum like TV/Facebook; growing platforms give far more).
3. Best version: **Give until they ask** — give in public, ask in private; let the audience self-select when ready to buy.
4. When you do ask, use **Integrated** offers (an ad inside every piece, ratio kept high) or **Intermittent** offers (e.g., 10 give posts then 1 ask). Advertise either your core offer or your lead magnet.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Asking too often shrinks the audience (a friend's podcast stopped growing then shrank). Short platforms favor intermittent; long-form favors integration. If unsure, ask for the lead magnet (lower risk).

## Cold Outreach Playbook (Big List + Big Fast Value + Volume)
**When to use**: When you've run out of warm audience or want faster, more predictable, private lead flow; the advanced cousin of warm outreach.
**How**: Solve the three problems strangers create —
1. **Get a targeted list** — (a) Software/scraping tools, (b) List brokers, (c) Elbow grease (join groups/communities). Build first 1000 names; self-assembled lists are freshest.
2. **Figure out what to say** — Personalize (act like you know them: 1-3 facts + a compliment) + Big Fast Value (give away something people actually pay for; blow minds in <30 sec). Use phone/email/DM scripts.
3. **Volume** — Automate delivery, automate distribution, and follow up more times in more ways (then restart the list after 3-6 months as circumstances change).
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Phone: 100 calls, 20% pickup, 25% take → 4 engaged leads. Email: 100 sent, 30% open, 10% reply → ~3% engaged (aim 3%). DM: ~20% reply. Win when LTGP ≥ 3× CAC (bare minimum; portfolio examples hit 10:1, even 30:1). Takes ~a year to scale; labor-intensive but reliable, private, hard to copy.

## The 4-Step Paid Ad
**When to use**: Fastest way to scale leads; trade money for guaranteed reach to cold audiences. Do this *last* of the core four (skills and money carry over).
**How**:
1. **Pick a platform** where 4 things are true: you've used/gotten value from it; you can target your people; you know how to format ads for it; you have the minimum spend.
2. **Target them** — use lookalike audiences (upload customers → warm → cold list to hit minimum) and/or factor targeting (age, income, interests, location). More filters = more efficient but burns faster.
3. **Make the ad** = Call Out + Value + CTA:
   - **Call Out** (most important; focus the first 5 seconds): Labels, Yes-Questions, If-Then Statements, Ridiculous Results; plus nonverbal Contrast, Likeness, The Scene.
   - **Value** via the What-Who-When Framework (8 elements: 4 value drivers + their opposites; from multiple Who perspectives; across past/present/future).
   - **CTA** — spell out exactly what to do; short, memorable; add urgency/scarcity/bonuses.
4. **Get permission to contact them** — a simple landing page matching the ad ("click to close" continuity); capture contact info.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Efficiency > creativity; "good enough is good enough." Start small (puddle → pond → lake → ocean); ratio drops as you scale but total profit rises. Focus the majority of effort on the first 5 seconds (made ads 20x more effective).

## The Three Phases of Scaling Paid Ads
**When to use**: Deciding how much to spend on ads.
**How**:
1. **Track Money** — set up accurate tracking before spending a dollar.
2. **Lose Money** ("investing in a money-printing machine") — budget 2× the 30-day cash collected per customer to test a new ad; kill it before 1× spend if getting no leads. Expect to lose more times than you win, but win big when you win (see winners, 10x down).
3. **Print Money** — once break-even or better, reverse the budget from your customer/sales goal (customers wanted × CAC, pad 20%) and commit.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: It costs money to build the machine (a business took a year to get profitable, then earned the year's "wasted" money back in the next month). If the number terrifies you, you're doing it right.

## Client-Financed Acquisition (CAC recouped in 30 days; 3:1 LTGP:CAC)
**When to use**: When ads are technically profitable over lifetime but the first purchase doesn't cover CAC, creating a cash-flow ceiling on scale.
**How**:
1. Measure LTGP-to-CAC (LTGP = all customer revenue minus cost to deliver). Aim for ≥ 3:1.
2. To fix it, make the customer pay back more than the cost to *get and fulfill* them within the first 30 days (any business can get 30-day interest-free money via credit card).
3. Immediately sell more stuff — e.g., a $100 upsell (100% margin) that 1 in 5 take adds $20/customer, taking $10 → $30 in 30 days against a $30 CAC = free customers.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Two levers — lower CAC (better ads) or raise LTGP (better business model); do both. If CAC < 3× industry average, fix the business model (LTGP); if > 3× average, fix advertising (CAC). Costs approach zero; revenue can go to infinity. Don't confuse a sales problem with an advertising problem (qualified leads not buying = sales problem).

## More / Better / New (Core Four On Steroids)
**When to use**: When you're doing the core four but want even more leads on your own; counters the "Size Of The Pie Fallacy."
**How**:
1. **More** — do more of what works (double inputs → more leads); the biggest gains come from volume.
2. **Better** — test to lift results for the same effort; find the constraint (the step with the biggest drop-off) and test there; test one thing per week per platform; log every test result.
3. **New** — when returns from more/better fall below what a new placement/platform would give, expand in this order: New placement → New platform → New core four activity.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Improving the constraint gives the biggest payoff (e.g., +5% on a 5% step doubles leads vs. +16% on a 30% step). One week is usually long enough to read a test; if you can't beat your best version in 4 tries (≈1 month), move to the next constraint. Exhaust more-better before new.

## The Rule of 100
**When to use**: Operationalizing "More" — a daily volume commitment that guarantees more engaged leads.
**How**: Do 100 primary actions per day, for 100 days straight, in your chosen core four method:
1. Warm Reach Outs: 100 reach outs/day.
2. Post Content: 100 minutes/day making content (release ≥1/day).
3. Cold Reach Outs: 100 reach outs/day (use automation; expect lower response).
4. Paid Ads: 100 minutes/day making ads + run them 100 days straight at your calculated daily budget (aim for Client-Financed Acquisition).
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: "Do 100 primary actions per day for 100 days straight and you will get more engaged leads." Volume is the most-underestimated variable.

## Open To Goal
**When to use**: Taking the Rule of 100 to the next level; for elite operators committing to outcomes, not just actions.
**How**:
1. Commit to working until you hit a specific number of *outcomes*, no matter what (e.g., 5 new members/day) — not a fixed number of attempts.
2. Structure the day to make it possible: wake early (4-5am), get right to work (no rituals), no meetings until noon (long uninterrupted block after long uninterrupted sleep).
3. Set the daily goal to match what you can do, then do the dedicated block before anything else.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: "Do more than they do, and you will have more than they have." May mean 12+ hour days; throttle back and build up if overwhelmed.

## Customer Referrals — Six Ways To Give Value + Seven Ways To Ask
**When to use**: To get the lowest-cost, highest-profit, best-quality leads (exponential, unlike linear core-four growth). Requires a product good enough to refer + actually asking.
**How** — Six ways to give more value (maps to ad parts):
1. Call Outs → Sell Better Customers (target the lowest-churn / best-result niche).
2. Dream Outcome → Set Better Expectations (lower promises to overdeliver).
3. Perceived Likelihood → Get More People Better Results (survey best customers → find common actions → force new customers to repeat them → match guarantee to those actions).
4. Time Delay → Make Faster Wins (more frequent wins/updates; BAMFAM; deliver early).
5. Effort & Sacrifice → Keep Making Your Stuff Better (monthly: find top complaint → fix → test → roll out).
6. Call To Action → Tell Them What To Buy Next (keep selling front and back end).

**How** — Seven ways to ask (treat referrals like an offer; show the value of referring):
1. One-Sided Referral Benefit (pay your CAC to referrer or friend).
2. Two-Sided Referral Benefits (split CAC both ways — Dropbox/PayPal).
3. Ask Right When They Buy (names/numbers on the contract; "people who do this with someone get 3x results").
4. Add Referrals As A Negotiation Chip (discount in exchange for 3 intros).
5. Referral Events ("Bring-a-Friend," "Spouse Challenge," 1-4 weeks).
6. Ongoing Referral Programs (talk about it always).
7. Unlockable Referral Bonuses (VIP, status, training — for referring + leaving a testimonial).
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Referrals > churn = grow with no other advertising. Dropbox 39x'd in 15 months; PayPal hit 100M users. The difference between price and value is goodwill — build goodwill, then capitalize with the asks. Test: "If all customers had to come from one customer, how would you treat them?"

## Internal Core Four (Recruiting Employees) + 3Ds Training
**When to use**: When workload exceeds one person; to build a business that runs (and is sellable) without you.
**How**: Map customer-getting to employee-getting (same skills):
- Warm Outreach → Asking Your Network; Cold Outreach → Recruiting; Post Content → Posting Job Openings; Paid Ads → Promoting Job Postings; Customer Referrals → Employee Referrals; Affiliates → Associations/Guilds/Listservs; Agencies → Staffing firms.
Then train with the **3Ds**:
1. **Document** — write the exact checklist; verify you can do an A+ job following only it.
2. **Demonstrate** — do it in front of them, adjusting the checklist where they slow down.
3. **Duplicate** — they do it in front of you following the checklist; fix the checklist until results match.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Trade ~40 hrs of doing for ~4 hrs of managing. Returns: Total Payroll / Total Engaged Leads = cost per lead (e.g., $100k/30k = $3.33). Fix the hiring bottleneck (interview in groups). Reward following directions; if directions are followed and results are wrong, fix the directions. A business that runs without you can be worth ~$10M+ (5x profit).

## Picking An Agency (and Using It The Right Way)
**When to use**: When you have money and want to learn a new method or new platform fast (skip trial-and-error), not to depend on them forever.
**How**:
1. Open every relationship with a purpose + deadline: "I want to do what you do… work with you ~6 months, pay extra for you to explain why you make each decision, then train my team and move to lower-cost consulting." (Negotiate; move on if they refuse.)
2. Optionally hire one "good enough" agency to learn the ropes + an elite agency (4x) to maximize.
3. Run both teams until yours beats theirs regularly, then drop to consulting, then cut them loose.
Pick agencies that check: (1) someone you know got results; (2) prominent clients; (3) a waiting list; (4) clear sales process with realistic expectations; (5) no short-term hacks; (6) tell you exactly what they need; (7) regular meeting cadence; (8) simple cost-vs-results tracking; (9) a good offer (dream outcome / likelihood / time / effort); (10) they're expensive (all good agencies are; not all expensive ones are good).
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Author cut team-beats-agency time from ~1 year → <6 months. Expect to pay agency + your team simultaneously for a stretch. One $6000 / 8-hour engagement taught a skill that made millions.

## Affiliate Army (6-Step Playbook)
**When to use**: Highest-leverage lead-getter; recruit independent businesses to advertise your stuff to their audiences (ALAN + Prestige Labs did $75M+ from 5000+ affiliates).
**How**:
1. **Find Your Ideal Affiliates** — businesses with a warm audience full of people like your customers ("Who's got my leads?"); categorize: softwares, products, equipment, services, groups, events.
2. **Make Them An Offer** — advertise like any offer (call out + value + CTA); the offer is a fast/simple/easy way to make commissions (make more from current customers, high likelihood, no build/deliver/support, start tomorrow).
3. **Qualify Them** — get them invested: Make Them A Customer (buy/use the product) and/or Make Them An Expert (paid certification, ~10-20% of what an active affiliate makes in year one).
4. **Figure Out What To Pay Them** — pay for new + repeat customers; base on max allowable CAC; use a 3-tier payout (Tier 1 25% on sign-up; Tier 2 50% on activation; Tier 3 100% on sustained performance) — blended payout < max CAC improves LTGP:CAC.
5. **Get Them Advertising — Launch** using Whisper (curiosity / call-outs, start years out) → Tease (value elements / What-Who-When) → Shout (CTA, bonuses, scarcity, urgency).
6. **Keep Them Advertising — Integrate** (easiest → hardest): affiliates give your lead magnet away free with their purchase; affiliates sell your lead magnet; affiliates sell your core offer directly.
**Trade-offs / Benchmarks**: Compare cost-to-get-an-affiliate vs. gross profit of all customers they bring (example: $4000 CAC → $54,000 left after goods + 40% payout = 12.5:1). Aim ≥ 3:1 (target 5:1, 10:1+). Treat affiliates like customers. Start by landing 10-20 affiliates, work out kinks, then scale.
