# Glossary — $100M Leads (Alex Hormozi)

**A-C-A (Acknowledge-Compliment-Ask)** — Reply framework for warm outreach: acknowledge what they said, compliment a positive trait, then ask a question steering toward your offer (Ch 5).

**Advertising** — The process of making known; letting strangers know about the stuff you sell. The skill that produces engaged leads (Ch 1).

**Affiliate** — An independent business (lead getter) that tells its own audience to buy your stuff in exchange for money, free stuff, or both (Ch 16).

**Affiliate Army** — A large recruited, activated, and integrated network of affiliates that produces compounding lead flow without you advertising (Ch 16).

**Agency** — A lead-getting service business you hire mainly to learn new methods/platforms, then transition to low-cost consulting once your team beats them (Ch 15).

**BAMFAM (Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting)** — Always schedule the next touchpoint during the current one so customers always know what happens next (Ch 13).

**Benchmarks** — Expected conversion rates per method (e.g., ~1 in 5 warm reach outs engage; ~3% email lead engagement) used to predict customers per 100 actions (Ch 5, Ch 8).

**Big Fast Value** — In cold outreach, blowing a stranger's mind in under 30 seconds by giving away something they'd normally pay for (Ch 8).

**CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer)** — Total advertising cost to get one paying customer (Ch 10).

**Call to Action (CTA)** — Telling the audience exactly what to do next, plus a reason to do it now (scarcity, urgency, any reason) (Ch 3, Ch 9).

**Callout** — Whatever you do (verbal or nonverbal) to grab the right audience's attention in an ad; the most important part (Ch 9).

**Client-Financed Acquisition** — Getting a customer to pay back more than it costs to acquire and fulfill them within 30 days, freeing cash to scale forever (Ch 10).

**Cocktail Party Effect** — The brain filtering noise to notice one relevant signal (like your name); the basis for effective callouts (Ch 9).

**Cold Audience** — Strangers who have NOT given permission to contact them (Ch 4).

**Cold Outreach** — 1-to-1 private contact with a cold audience; solve: get a way to contact, what to say, contact until ready (Ch 8).

**Constraint** — The step where the most leads drop off; the highest-leverage point to test and improve (Ch 11).

**Content Unit** — Smallest material that can Hook, Retain, and Reward attention; link units to make longer content (Ch 6).

**Core Four** — The only four ways to let people know about your stuff: Warm Outreach, Post Content, Cold Outreach, Paid Ads (Ch 4).

**Core Offer** — The main thing you sell for money; the direct path to a sale (Ch 3).

**Customer Referral** — A referrer (best: a customer) sending you an engaged lead; lowest-cost, highest-profit, exponential leads (Ch 13).

**Engaged Lead** — A person who *shows* interest in the stuff you sell; the true output of advertising (Ch 2).

**Give-Give-Give-Ask** — Over-give value in content and ask rarely; ideally "give until they ask" to grow fastest (Ch 7).

**Give Away the Secrets, Sell the Implementation** — Make your lead magnet as good as paid stuff; provide more value than the core offer costs before they buy (Ch 3).

**Goodwill** — The gap between value delivered and price charged (customer surplus); the fuel for word of mouth and referrals (Ch 13).

**Grand Slam Offer** — An offer so good people feel stupid saying no; works for free lead magnets too (Ch 1, Ch 3).

**Hidden Costs** — The time, effort, and sacrifice required to get results (the bottom of the value equation); make "free" feel expensive if too high (Ch 5).

**Hook-Retain-Reward** — The three jobs of content: get noticed, get consumed, satisfy why they consumed it (Ch 6).

**Integrated vs. Intermittent Offers** — Two ways to monetize content: ask inside each piece (long-form) or every Nth piece (short-form) (Ch 7).

**Integration (Affiliates)** — The long-term strategy to keep affiliates advertising: give away, sell your lead magnet, or sell your core offer inside their offer (Ch 16).

**Internal Core Four** — Using the Core Four to get employees (warm=network, cold=recruiting, content=job postings, ads=promoted postings) (Ch 14).

**Lead** — A person you can contact. That's all (Ch 2).

**Lead Getter** — Someone who gets leads for you: Customers, Employees, Agencies, Affiliates; the leverage to a $100M machine (Ch 12).

**Lead Magnet** — A complete solution to a narrow problem (free/low-cost) that reveals a bigger problem your core offer solves (Ch 3).

**Leverage** — How much you get for the time you spend; lead getters maximize it (Ch 12).

**LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)** — All money a customer ever spends minus all cost to deliver; used instead of LTV (Ch 10).

**LTGP : CAC Ratio** — Efficiency measure of advertising; aim for at least 3:1 (Ch 10).

**Many-Sided Die** — Closing parable: keep rolling (advertising); every die hits its green streak if rolled enough — you can't lose if you don't quit (Ch 17).

**More, Better, New** — Three ways to boost any Core Four method: do more, do it better (test the constraint), do it somewhere new (Ch 11).

**Nine-Word Email** — Dean Jackson's re-engagement template: "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?" (Ch 5).

**Open to Goal** — Working until a specific *outcome* is hit no matter what (vs. a set number of actions); Rule of 100 for the big kids (Ch 17).

**One-Page Advertising Checklist** — A 5-step daily plan: pick lead type, pick Rule of 100 or Open to Goal, fill checklist, do it daily, then hire and repeat (Ch 17).

**Paid Ads** — 1-to-many to a cold audience; pay to put your offer in front of others' audiences. Fastest reach; a game of efficiency (Ch 9).

**Personalization** — Researching 1-3 facts about a stranger so cold outreach feels warm (Ch 8).

**Post Free Content** — 1-to-many to a warm audience; grow a compounding audience (the asset is the audience, not the content) (Ch 6).

**Problem-Solution Cycle** — Every problem has a solution; every solution reveals a new problem — pick a narrow problem your core offer's next problem solves (Ch 3).

**Reveal / Sample-Trial / One-Step** — The three lead-magnet types: reveal a problem, give a sample/trial, or give one step of a multi-step process (Ch 3).

**Roadmap (7 Levels)** — Seven levels of advertisers from "friends know" to hiring killer executives, scaling toward $100M (Ch 17).

**Rule of 100** — Do 100 primary advertising actions (or 100 minutes) per day for 100 days straight (Ch 11).

**Size of the Pie Fallacy** — Mistaking your tiny advertised slice for the whole market; markets are far bigger than assumed (Ch 11).

**Three Phases of Scaling Ads** — Track Money, Lose Money, Print Money (Ch 10).

**Three Ds (Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate)** — Training model: write the checklist, do it in front of them, have them do it in front of you (Ch 14).

**Value Equation** — Four value elements: Dream Outcome, Perceived Likelihood of Achievement, Time Delay, Effort & Sacrifice; maximize the first two, minimize the last two (Ch 5).

**Warm Audience** — People who gave permission to contact them: friends, family, followers, customers (Ch 4).

**Warm Outreach** — 1-to-1 private contact with a warm audience; cheapest, most reliable way to get first leads (Ch 5).

**What-Who-When Framework** — Ad copy lens: What (8 value elements + opposites), Who (status across people they know), When (past/present/future) (Ch 9).

**Whisper-Tease-Shout** — Three-phase launch method to activate affiliates/audience: build curiosity, reveal value, then drive action (Ch 16).
