# Cheatsheet — Competing Against Luck (Jobs to Be Done)

## The one question
**"What job did you hire that product to do?"** — customers *hire* products to make progress and *fire* them when something does the job better.

## The definition (memorize the three words)
**A Job = the PROGRESS a person is trying to make in a particular CIRCUMSTANCE** — with **Functional + Social + Emotional** dimensions.
- The **circumstance**, not the customer, is the unit of innovation.
- Jobs are stable; the products hired for them churn.

## Write the job
"When **[circumstance]**, help me **[verb: progress]**, so I can **[deeper aim]**."
→ Verbs & nouns only (never adjectives/adverbs). Right altitude: broad enough to see real competition, narrow enough to design.

## A job is NOT
A "need" · a customer attribute/demographic · a one-time event · a product category · an adjective (a nice experience).

## The Forces of Progress (switch happens only if ↓)
| Driving (amplify) | Opposing (dismantle) |
|---|---|
| **Push** of the situation | **Habits** of the present |
| **Pull** of the new solution | **Anxiety** (of choosing + of use) |

**push + pull > habit + anxiety.** Most teams over-invest in *pull* and ignore the two opposing forces — reducing anxiety/habit is often the cheaper win.

## Big Hire vs Little Hire
**Big Hire** = the purchase. **Little Hire** = every actual use. Bought-but-unused → the job isn't really being done.

## Where to hunt for jobs (5)
1. A job **close to home** (your own life) · 2. **Nonconsumption** ("compete with nothing") · 3. **Workarounds / compensating behaviors** · 4. **Negative jobs** (what they refuse to do) · 5. **Unusual / off-label uses**. → Watch behavior, not stated preference.

## From insight to product
**Job spec** (dimensions · trade-offs · competing set · obstacles & anxieties to remove) → **product as résumé** (answers the spec line-by-line) → **experiences of purchase & use** (deliver social/emotional job; hard to copy) → **purpose brand** (brand = the job; commands premium).

## Moat: integrate around the job
Advantage = **processes**, not resources/features (rivals copy those). Build processes *across silos* to deliver the whole job (Mayo Clinic). *"If you can't describe it as a process, you don't know what you're doing."*

## The Three Fallacies of Innovation Data (why firms drift)
1. **Active vs. Passive Data** — easy ops data crowds out rich job/context data.
2. **Surface Growth** — selling more products to existing customers dilutes the core job.
3. **Conforming Data** — bending data to confirm what you already believe.
Test every metric: *"Customer's progress, or my internal activity?"*

## Jobs-focused organization
Job = **commander's intent** → distributed decisions · resource optimization · inspiration · **better measurement**. *What gets measured gets done* → measure progress on the customer's job.

## Punchlines
- "People don't want a quarter-inch drill; they want a quarter-inch hole." (and the hole is a proxy for deeper progress)
- Correlation tells you *what*; the **job** tells you *why* — and *why* is what predicts.
- Innovation feels like luck only because firms measure customer/product *attributes*, which don't cause purchases.
