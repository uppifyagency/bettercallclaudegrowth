# Glossary — Competing Against Luck (Christensen, Hall, Dillon & Duncan)

**Active data** — Operations/sales data the company generates after launch; abundant, quantified, framed in the firm's own categories. Easy to collect, and about the *product*, not the job (Ch 8).

**Anxiety of choosing** — Pre-purchase fear that the new solution won't deliver / will waste money; one of the forces opposing a switch (Ch 5).

**Anxiety of use** — Fear of not being able to learn/operate the new solution, or of looking foolish; the second face of new-solution anxiety (Ch 5).

**Big Hire** — The purchase moment: money changes hands and the product comes home (Ch 5).

**Causality (vs. correlation)** — The mechanism of *why* a customer hires a product. Jobs Theory supplies causality; data supplies only correlation (Ch 1, 2, 8, 10).

**Circumstance** — The situation that defines a job; the fundamental unit of innovation work — not the customer's attributes (Ch 2).

**Commander's intent** — A clear statement of purpose that lets people improvise correctly without orders; a well-defined job plays this role in a firm (Ch 9).

**Competing against nonconsumption** — Targeting people who currently hire *nothing* (or a workaround) for the job; the largest growth opportunity (Ch 3, 4).

**Compensating behavior / workaround** — A jury-rigged hack people use when no good solution exists; a behavioral signal of an unmet job (Ch 4).

**Conforming data (Fallacy of)** — Unconsciously shaping data to confirm existing beliefs; the third innovation-data fallacy (Ch 8).

**Constructs** — The building-block concepts (job, progress, forces, Big/Little Hire) that make the causal theory usable (Ch 10).

**Emotional dimension** — How the customer wants to *feel* while making progress; a core part of every job (Ch 2).

**Experiences (of purchase and use)** — The touchpoints that deliver the social/emotional job and reduce anxiety; hard to copy, source of premium and loyalty (Ch 6).

**Fire** — To stop using/replace a product because something does the job better (or the job changed) (Ch 1).

**Forces of Progress** — The four forces governing a switch: push + pull (driving) vs. habit + anxiety (opposing). Switch happens only when push+pull > habit+anxiety (Ch 5).

**Functional dimension** — The practical task to be accomplished; the most visible (but rarely sufficient) part of a job (Ch 2).

**Hire** — To buy and use a product to make progress on a job (Ch 1).

**Habits of the present** — Inertia and comfort with the current solution; a force opposing change (Ch 5).

**Job (to be done)** — *The progress a person is trying to make in a particular circumstance*, with functional, social & emotional dimensions (Ch 2).

**Job spec** — The innovator's blueprint of the job: its dimensions, trade-offs, competing set, and obstacles/anxieties to remove (Ch 6).

**Little Hire** — Each actual use of the product to get the job done; without it the Big Hire is hollow (Ch 5).

**Negative job** — Something the customer emphatically does *not* want to do; eliminating it is a rich opportunity (Ch 4).

**Nonconsumption** — The state of not hiring anything for a job; "competing with nothing" (Ch 3, 4).

**Passive data** — Rich, qualitative, hard-to-collect information about the customer's job and circumstance; crowded out by active data (Ch 8).

**Processes** — How a firm integrates to get the job done; invisible, hard to copy — the durable competitive advantage (Ch 7).

**Progress** — Movement toward a goal/aspiration; the heart of a job (always a verb-driven struggle) (Ch 2).

**Pull (of the new)** — The attraction of a new solution that promises the progress; a driving force (Ch 5).

**Purpose brand** — A brand made mentally synonymous with a job; signals to customers and guides employees; commands premium prices (Ch 6).

**Push (of the situation)** — The frustration/triggering events that make the status quo intolerable; a driving force (Ch 5).

**Quarter-inch hole** — Levitt's reminder that customers hire the drill for the hole — and even the hole is a proxy for deeper progress (Ch 8).

**Résumé (product as)** — The product designed backward from the job spec, "applying for the job" with features and experiences as its credentials (Ch 6).

**Social dimension** — How the customer wants to be *perceived by others* while making progress; part of every job (Ch 2).

**Storyboarding the struggle** — Reconstructing the customer's decision frame-by-frame (first thought → active looking → purchase → use) to hear what they don't say (Ch 5).

**Struggling moment** — The specific circumstance + triggering event that starts the hiring process (Ch 5).

**Surface growth (Fallacy of)** — Chasing growth by selling more products to existing customers, diluting focus on the core job; the second data fallacy (Ch 8).

**Three Fallacies of Innovation Data** — Active-vs-passive, surface growth, conforming data: how growing firms lose sight of the job (Ch 8).
